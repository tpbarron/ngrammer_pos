<p>
<s>the may jobs report shows that employers added only 69000 jobs while unemployment rose to 82</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  businesses hired shockingly few workers in may throwing into doubt the strength of the economic recovery</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>only 69000 jobs were added last month the weakest growth in a year</s>
<s>the unemployment rate rose to 82 the first uptick since last june</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>economists surveyed by cnnmoney had expected to see employers add 150000 jobs and the unemployment rate to remain at 81</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>revisions from previous months also showed the economy gained 49000 fewer jobs in march and april than originally thought</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>private companies sharply cut back on hiring last month adding only a meager 82000 jobs</s>
<s>employment increased in health care transportation and wholesale trade</s>
<s>manufacturing continued to add positions albeit at a slower pace than earlier this year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>construction however suffered its largest decline in two years</s>
<s>the public sector continued to shed jobs losing 13000 in may</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the overall figure stunned most economists who laid the blame for the slow growth at the feet of europe and washington dc</s>
<s>the steady drumbeat of headlines concerning europes financial woes as well as the looming fiscal cliff in the us is weighing on consumer confidence</s>
<s>adding to the problems is the slowdown in the chinese economy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it was really shockingly low said bill dunkelberg chief economist for the national federation of independent business who said small businesses have pulled back from hiring because they arent getting more customers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>meanwhile the socalled underemployment rate rose to 148 after falling or holding steady so far this year</s>
<s>some experts say this rate is a better measure of the job markets health since it also includes people who could only get parttime positions and who have looked for a job at some point in the past year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rising unemployment rate contained a glimmer of good news</s>
<s>it increased because 642000 people reentered the labor force often a sign of economic optimism</s>
<s>economists attribute the jump to graduating college students looking for jobs earlier as well as jobs created at startup businesses not captured by survey of established employers which produces the closelyfollowed payroll number</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>still the report marks a major turnaround from the beginning of the year</s>
<s>job growth started off strong in the first two months of 2012 thanks in part to warmer weather that boosted hiring in construction and some other industries</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but it now seems clear that the economy is not strong enough to sustain monthly job growth that exceeds 200000 economists said</s>
<s>gross domestic product the broadest measure of the nations economic health grew at an annual rate of 19 in the first quarter significantly slower than the 3 growth rate at the end of last year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as a result employers dont feel the need to boost their payrolls</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its not like businesses are being swamped with demands for goods and services said sean snaith an economist with the university of central florida</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at the same time companies are being very cautious because of the events in the us and abroad</s>
<s>that isnt likely to change anytime soon especially with the us presidential election looming in november</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>whenever uncertainty abounds its hard to open the floodgates on hiring said ellen zentner senior economist at nomura securities</s>
<s>i would expect to see anemic hiring persist over the next few months</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but the overall job market still has a long way to go to climb out of the deep hole left by the financial crisis</s>
<s>of the 88 million jobs lost only about 38 million have been added back</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>roughly 127 million americans remain unemployed and 428 of them have been so for six months or more</s>
<s>the number of longterm unemployed rose to 54 million up from 51 million</s>
</p>
