<p>
<s>the sec wants to overhaul moneymarket funds</s>
<s>for now there are better choices</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  moneymarket funds used to be boring  and investors loved them that way</s>
<s>you could earn bankbeating yields with little risk even if the funds werent backed by fdic insurance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>then came the financial crisis which put the locktight safety of some funds in doubt followed by rockbottom yields</s>
<s>a years interest on $10000 in a money fund might buy you a latte</s>
<s>thanks federal reserve</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>now the securities and exchange commission is talking about new rules for moneymarket funds which could make you rethink where you put money you want to keep safe</s>
<s>but for the time being youre often better off holding cash elsewhere</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>moneymarket funds as opposed to bank moneymarket accounts are mutual funds that invest in lowrisk securities and aim for a steady net asset value of $1 per share  meaning your principal shouldnt waver</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the crisis a wellknown fund dropped below $1</s>
<s>investors then pulled out of other money funds risking a nonbank bank run if funds had to sell assets fast</s>
<s>so washington gave funds a temporary fdiclike guarantee which ran out in 2009</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money 70 best mutual funds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>now the sec is considering eliminating the $1pershare expectation</s>
<s>instead you would see funds value float like stock and bond funds do</s>
<s>the thinking is that shareholders might be less likely to race for the door at the first sign of a dip below $1</s>
<s>and investors would also get a signal that even if a moneymarket fund is very low risk you cant think of it as a bank</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>another proposal would make funds keep a capital buffer and hold back 3 of investors money for 30 days after redemption</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fund companies are lobbying hard against these ideas but its likely that some new rules will be adopted eventually says peter crane of moneyfund tracker crane data</s>
<s>while dc squabbles heres how to handle your cash</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>banks are the best bet now</s>
<s>fdicinsured savings and moneymarket accounts pay higher yields than money funds at the moment</s>
<s>money funds can still be convenient for brokerage sweep accounts or to funnel money into mutual funds</s>
<s>but limit them to 2 of your portfolio says odenton md adviser jim ludwick</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>when interest rates rise move in</s>
<s>youll see a faster yield uptick in moneymarket funds than in banks</s>
<s>caveat theyll probably be slower than they used to be to pass along higher rates says mike krasner managing editor of imoneynet</s>
<s>thats partly because of tighter investment rules the sec has already put in place and partly because fund companies want to make up fees they waived during this ultralowrate era</s>
<s>just one more hangover from the financial crisis</s>
</p>
