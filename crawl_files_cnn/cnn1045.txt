<p>
<s>american apparel inc which manufactures army uniforms in alabama has laid off 150 workers as a result of going headtohead with unicor for government contracts</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  just hearing the word unicor is enough to make kurt wilson see red</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unicor is a governmentrun enterprise that employs over 13000 inmates  at wages as low as 23 cents an hour  to make goods for the pentagon and other federal agencies</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with some exceptions unicor gets first dibs on federal contracts over private companies as long as its bid is comparable in price quantity and delivery</s>
<s>in other words if unicor wants a contract it gets it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>and that makes wilson and other small business owners angry</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>wilson has been competing with unicor for 20 years</s>
<s>hes an executive at american apparel inc an alabama company that makes military uniforms</s>
<s>it is not affiliated with the international retailer of the same name</s>
<s> he has gone headtohead with unicor on just about every product his company makes  and said he has laid off 150 people over the years as a result</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we pay employees $9 on average wilson said</s>
<s>they get full medical insurance 401k plans and paid vacation</s>
<s>yet were competing against a federal program that doesnt pay any of that</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unicor also known as federal prison industries is part of the us bureau of prisons</s>
<s>it has been preparing inmates for jobs after they get out since 1934</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the program has 83 factories and makes goods in seven industries  apparel being the biggest ticket</s>
<s>unicor made over $900 million in revenue last year and faces more heat from businesses and lawmakers as the economy takes a toll on small manufacturers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related can the us handle a manufacturing comeback</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in olive hill ky apparel factory ashland sales and service co has been making windbreakers for the air force for 14 years says michael mansh who runs the factory</s>
<s>last february when he learned that unicor was eyeing the contract he reached out to kentucky sen mitch mcconnell</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mcconnell one of the top republicans on capitol hill issued a public statement urging unicor to back off</s>
<s>the next day it did</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with 100 employees mansh said ashland is olive hills largest employer</s>
<s>and he said losing the air force contract would have shut the factory down</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>thats 100 people buying groceries</s>
<s>we use trucking companies in the town buy parts and light bulbs there every day he said</s>
<s>thats all lost when prisons take away contracts</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unicor is not required to pay its workers minimum wage and instead pays inmates 23 cents to $115 an hour</s>
<s>it doesnt have health insurance costs</s>
<s>it also doesnt shell out federal state or local taxes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related fiscal cliff threatens small businesses</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>advocates for private sector companies are loudly campaigning for reform of unicors preferential status</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unemployment has been over 8 for nearly four years and theres a federal program tanking our industry said kurt courtney director of government relations at the american apparel and footwear association</s>
<s>the only way for workers to get jobs back is to go to prison</s>
<s>theres got to be a better way to do this</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in 2008 congress amended the law to limit unicors advantage for certain kinds of pentagon contracts</s>
<s>now a bill in the house supported by 28 lawmakers from both parties would go further and require unicor to compete across the board</s>
<s>the bill also provides alternative ways for training inmates who would instead work for charities religious organizations local governments or school districts</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we know that in the recovery many new jobs are coming out of small businesses said rep bill huizenga a michigan republican who introduced the bill</s>
<s>it makes no sense to strangle them in the cradle</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>huizenga expects a similar bill to be introduced in the senate in the coming months</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unicor doesnt agree with the criticism</s>
<s>according to spokeswoman julie rozier inmates working for unicor are 24 less likely to reoffend and 14 more likely to be employed longterm upon release</s>
<s>she also noted that over 40 of unicors supplies were purchased from small businesses in 2011</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>she cited the unique costs associated with operating within a prison</s>
<s>for example unicor employs more supervisors than a private sector firm would and security lockdowns disrupt production</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>businesses arent buying it</s>
<s>john palatiello president of the business coalition for fair competition said his organization of businesses and taxpayer groups is sympathetic to unicors goals</s>
<s>but they shouldnt be accomplished at the expense of small businesses</s>
</p>

<s>who is being punished here</s>
<s> he said</s>
<s>the inmates who have committed a crime against society or the employees of private companies who play by the rules</s>
