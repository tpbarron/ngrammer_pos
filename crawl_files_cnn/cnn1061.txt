<p>
<s>antonis samaras leader of new democracy is expected to be sworn in as greeces next prime minister</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  greek politicians reached a deal wednesday to form a new government that promises to uphold the nations financial commitments while also seeking to renegotiate its unpopular bailout agreement</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the conservative new democracy party which won the most votes in sundays election will form a coalition with the socialist pasok party and the smaller democratic left party</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new democracy leader antonis samaras was sworn in as prime minister wednesday afternoon in athens</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the new government takes over at a time when greece is facing deep political and economic uncertainty</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new democracy and pasok both signed off on the bailout agreement greece struck in march with the nations international creditors</s>
<s>but the austere terms attached to the emergency funding pushed greece to the brink of political collapse in may</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>syriza the farleft political party that rejects austerity came in second in sundays election</s>
<s>syriza leader alexis tsiparis met with samaras but would not back the coalition</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the new democracyled government is now widely expected to renegotiate the terms of the bailout although it remains committed to honoring the deal and remaining in the euro area</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the key message of this election is that greece decided to maintain the european trajectory and to stay in eurozone said dimitrios tsomocos an oxford economist and advisor to samaras</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>tsomocos said the coalition government is committed to the structural reforms greece must make to restore economic competitiveness</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but he added that new democracy will work with the europeans to modify and enhance the bailout agreement to include growthenhancing policies</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>there are signs that the european union international monetary fund and european central bank collectively known as the troika are willing to make concessions</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in a statement issued sunday the eurogroup of euro area finance ministers said the troika looks forward to an exchange of views with the greek government once it is formed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the immediate goal is to give greece more time to meet its fiscal targets including a deadline at the end of june to identify additional budget cuts</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>however allowing greece more time to meet its targets could cause the government to run in arrears meaning it will go deeper into debt</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in addition the greek economy has taken a turn for the worse since the bailout agreement was signed in march</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the combination of weakerthanexpected economic performance and additional debt means greece could soon need more bailout money</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the government hopes that its focus on boosting economic growth will mitigate any new funding needs by generating new revenue tsomocos said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>analysts at political research firm eurasia group estimate that greece could have between €20 billion to €30 billion in additional funding needs depending on how the terms are restructured</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the renegotiation process is expected to take at least three weeks</s>
<s>but it could take longer depending on how things play out in the netherlands</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the dutch government collapsed in april and the nation may not agree to any concessions for greece until after elections are held in midseptember</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>there is a chance that negotiations could drag out until the end of august when greece is due to make a €31 billion bond payment</s>
<s>but analysts say greece and the eu would likely find a way to make the payment possibly through a loan from the eurozone bailout fund</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while there is quite a bit of wiggle room to renegotiate the bailout the process is not going to be easy said to nicholas spiro director of londonbased consultancy spiro sovereign strategy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the political haggling between both sides is likely to be drawnout messy and could easily be the trigger for the collapse of the government spiro wrote in a note to clients</s>
</p>

<s>the athens composite ath edged higher along with the global x ftse greece 20 etf grek</s>
<s>but the national bank of greece nbg which has surged 70 over the past five days took the classic sell on the news approach sliding 6</s>
</p>
