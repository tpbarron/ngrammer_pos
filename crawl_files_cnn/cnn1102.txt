<s>new york cnnmoney  spain is fast becoming the biggest threat to europes shared currency as the cashstrapped government struggles to contain a growing banking crisis</s>

<p>
<s>the depth of spains banking crisis was exposed late last week after the government announced a €19 billion rescue of one of the nations top lenders bankia</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the move raised worries that spanish banks face largerthanexpected losses on bad loans stemming from the collapse of the nations housing bubble</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but the spanish government does not have the money to bail out the entire banking sector which analysts say could cost upwards of €100 billion</s>
<s>madrid has already warned that it will miss its targets for deficit reduction this year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the combination of mounting losses in the banking sector and the governments limited financial resources has raised fears spain may need to be rescued</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spain is now seemingly drifting inexorably to a bailout of one sort or other said emily nicol an economist daiwa capital markets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the markets yields on spanish bonds have increased as investors demand higher interest rates to assume the lending risk</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the yield on 10year spanish bonds rose to a high of 668 this week</s>
<s>thats approaching the level that ultimately led to bailouts for other struggling euro area nations such as greece ireland and portugal</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while spain is not on the verge of insolvency the government cannot afford to pay interest rates at current levels for very long</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spanish economy minister luis de guindos warned this week that current yields are not very sustainable over the long term according to reports</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in addition to the banks spain is potentially on the hook for the debts of several regional governments</s>
<s>on thursday ratings agency fitch downgraded eight spanish autonomous communities citing largerthanexpected deficits and rising borrowing costs</s>

<p>
<s>the european union has increased its crisis resources but the cost to bail out spain could quickly burn through the regions firewall</s>

<p>
<s>the european stability mechanism a bailout fund that comes into effect this summer will be equipped with €500 billion</s>

<p>

<s>theres probably not enough political will in europe to do both</s>

<p>
<s>eu officials could allow the esm to lend directly to banks which would take some of the pressure off of the spanish government to recapitalize the banks</s>
