<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  the leaders of the four biggest eurozone economies agreed friday on a plan to boost economic growth in the troubled currency union</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at a meeting in rome the leaders of germany france italy and spain discussed their priorities for a key summit of european union leaders next week</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>without going into detail the leaders said they agreed on a set of growthenhancing policies equal to about €125 billion or 1 of eurozone gross domestic product</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we are preparing a plan for economic integration on a longterm basis in europe said italian prime minister mario monti at a press conference following the meeting</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>monti was flanked by german chancellor angela merkel french president francois hollande and spanish prime minister mariano rajoy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the meeting came ahead of the next european council which brings together the leaders of all 27 members of the european union on june 2829</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on friday the leaders said they plan to outline their longterm vision for the euro currency and take steps to strengthen the monetary union with deeper economic and financial integration</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in particular the leaders are expected to announce the formation of a banking union which could include shared oversight and deposit insurance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>they are also considering an increase in the resources of the european investment bank</s>
<s>the goal is to boost growth and create jobs by investing in public works</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at the press conference the leaders did not say anything specific about a controversial plan to use bailout funds to buy euro area government bonds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>merkel has resisted the proposal which is backed by monti hollande and rajoy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>germany is generally opposed to the idea of using eurozone funds to subsidize government debt since doing so could make it easier for policymakers to delay unpopular economic reforms</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>however borrowing costs for large euro area economies including italy and spain have been rising as investors fear those nations may need to be bailed out</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the leaders did express support for a tax on financial transactions a move championed by hollande</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hollande reiterated that eurobonds which would combine the debts of euro area governments are one of many useful instruments under consideration</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>rajoy seemed to support using funds from the european financial stability facility to recapitalize spanish banks</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spain will officially request loans from the euro area bailout fund to shore up insolvent banks on monday</s>
<s>final terms and conditions of the loans still need be finalized</s>
</p>
