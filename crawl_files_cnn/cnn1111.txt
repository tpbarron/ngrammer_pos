<p>

</p>

<p>
<s>next up for the rating agency are banks in the uk as well as 17 major banks with global capital markets operations</s>
<s> that includes wall street firms citigroup c fortune 500 jpmorgan chase jpm fortune 500 bank of america bac fortune 500 goldman sachs gs fortune 500 and morgan stanley ms fortune 500</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the other rating agencies standard  poors and fitch cut their ratings on some of the largest us banks at the end of last year</s>
<s>banks have been trying to assure investors theyre ready for any further rating downgrades ever since moodys first announced a review of the sector back in february</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>morgan stanley ceo james gorman told investors at a conference this week that even if it is downgraded by three notches by moodys it would be a manageable outcome due to the banks liquidity</s>

<p>
<s>the banks have been addressing this</s>
<s>they knew it was coming said nancy bush banking analyst for snl</s>
<s>financial a research firm that follows the financial sector</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but bush said the downgrades would raise costs for the major banks since theyll have to pay more to raise the cash they need to operate daytoday</s>
</p>

<p>

</p>

<p>
<s>at worst some banks may find themselves unable to sell their commercial paper to money market funds</s>
<s>commercial paper is a form of very shortterm borrowing banks and other companies use to meet their overnight cash needs</s>
<s>money market funds the most significant buyer of commercial paper have limits on the debt they can buy based on the credit rating of the companies offering it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it may also become more difficult for consumers and businesses to borrow money if banks decide they need to hold onto their cash in order to meet their liquidity needs bush said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it certainly does not help the cause of easing credit she said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the uk and major us banks are likely to be downgraded even though neither country is in the eurozone</s>
<s>the international banking system is so interconnected that there is no way those banks can be spared some pain from problems in the weaker european countries and banking system</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in fact that was the main reason for the downgrade of the dutch banks</s>
<s>moodys said they generally retained good access to market funding and asset quality remains sound to date</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but their exposure to the european sovereign debt crisis coupled with the fact that the fact that the netherlands is in a recession and is suffering from declining home prices led to the downgrade</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fridays move follows actions by moodys on banks across europe over the past month including ratings cuts of german and austrian banks last week and downgrades of banks in denmark sweden and finland at the end of may</s>
