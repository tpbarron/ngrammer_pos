<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  fitch ratings downgraded minnesotas debt ratings in the wake of a government shutdown stemming from a budget dispute</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fitch knocked the state off its triplea rating pedestal because of its difficulties in reaching consensus on its budget gap for the fiscal year that began july 1</s>
<s>fitch also cited the increasingly contentious budgeting environment in the state in recent years</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a lower debt rating hurts minnesota because the increased risk means it will need to pay higher interest rates to investors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the state shutdown all but essential government services on july 1 after governor mark dayton a democrat and republican lawmakers failed to make peace over their budget dispute and fill a $14 billion gap</s>
<s>theyre fighting over whether to raise taxes or cut spending</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this caused the state parks to close down throughout the independence day weekend and triggered the layoffs of more than 20000 state employees</s>
<s>road construction projects are on hold and highway rest stops are shuttered</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rating agency also highlighted minnesotas reliance on onetime budget fixes during the recession and the likelihood that any budget accord will contain more of them</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fitch said the most significant of these measures were payment deferrals to school districts and the use of funds from the federal stimulus program</s>
<s>the $14 billion budget gap represents school aid payment shifts employed to balance the last biennial budget said fitch</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the ratings agency now has a doubleaplus rating for minnesota debt</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on a positive note fitch praised minnesota for its balanced economy with a recovering job market</s>
<s>the agency also said the state has a strong debt position with a debt burden that is on the low end of the moderate range at about 27 of personal income</s>
</p>
