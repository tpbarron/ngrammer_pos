<p>
<s>spanish bond yields are still trading at anxietyinducing heights and investors cant stop thinking about the possiblity of a bailout</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  spains borrowing costs continued to rise tuesday with the yield on the 10year bond hitting another record high above 7</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>worries that spain may need a bailout of its own is fueling investor anxiety</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the yield on the 10year bond spiked to 727 early tuesday  its highest level since the euro debuted in 1999  according to tradeweb before easing slightly to 711</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the 7 mark is the first warning sign that a country may be headed toward needing financial rescue</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it looks inevitable that spain is going to mirror the experience of italy portugal and greece and is going to need a bailout sooner than later said nick stamenkovic fixed income strategist at ria capital markets in edinburgh scotland</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rising borrowing costs came as spain sold €24 billion worth of oneyear bills at 52 and €639 million of 18month bills at 535</s>
<s>while the demand was strong the yields were nearly twice as high as last months auction</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spain cannot afford to borrow at 7 for long and analysts say the nation is effectively shut out of the market at these levels</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rising rates signal investors total lack of confidence in spains sovereign debt especially considering the relatively short maturities of the bills said stamenkovic</s>
<s>inevitably that means spain will probably need a full scale bailout in addition to the banking bailout by autumn if not earlier</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on june 7 credit rating agency fitch downgraded the countrys sovereign debt to the lowest grade above junk status</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>two days later the spanish government requested up to €100 billion from the european union to recapitalize insolvent banks</s>
<s>but the rescue plan failed to ease concerns about the sustainability of spains public debt</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>an audit of spanish banks is due to be released thursday  the same day spain plans to auction off longerterm bonds</s>
<s>but a more indepth audit that was due at the end of july has been pushed to september</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we believe sustained high yields will eventually force spain into taking a fullfledged bailout wrote brown brothers harriman strategist marc chandler in a client note</s>
<s>the market simply does not have this kind of patience</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on tuesday banking stocks banco santander san and bbva bbva both closed higher by about 4</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>european stocks rallied</s>
<s>londons ftse ukx the dax dax in frankfurt and the cac 40 cac40 all ended about 17 higher </s>
