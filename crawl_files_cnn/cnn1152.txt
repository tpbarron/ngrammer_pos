<p>
<s>bank of england governor mervyn king announces steps to boost bank lending</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the bank of england and the chancellor of the exchequer are working on new policies to support the british economy boe governor mervyn king said thursday</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with signs of a deterioration in the outlook especially in world markets the case for a further monetary easing is growing king said in a speech to business leaders in london</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>king said he is working with chancellor george osborne on a funding for lending scheme to help spur bank lending and stimulate economic activity</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in present circumstances when private sector spending is depressed by extreme uncertainty there may be a case for a scheme to underwrite risks which the market itself is unwilling to take said king</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the scheme would provide funding to banks for several years at rates below current market rates he said</s>
<s>the funding would be linked to the banks performance in lending to the real economy during the present period of heightened uncertainty</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the program could support an estimated 80 billion pounds in new lending sources told the financial times</s>
<s>in addition the boe will activate a sixmonth liquidity program friday for banks to borrow no less than 5 billion pounds a month the newspaper said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the goal is to tackle the high level of funding costs directly said king</s>
<s>he hopes the plan will be in place within a few weeks</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>speaking at the same event osborne said the program will inject new confidence into our financial system and support the flow of credit to where it is needed in the real economy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we are not powerless in the face of the eurozone debt storm osborne added</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>osborne said euro area nations need to move towards greater political and economic integration to make the currency union sustainable</s>
<s>but it may take greek exit to make it happen he added</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>greek voters will return to the polls sunday in a repeat of last months inconclusive parliamentary election</s>
<s>the election is being framed as a potential catalyst for greece to exit the euro area</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that is a decision for the greek people said osborne</s>
<s>a time for decision has come</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>osborne said he had doubts about the euro project from the start recalling that his party opposed membership in the currency union</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but we should all be clear that it is strongly in britains interests for our biggest trading partners to succeed the chancellor said</s>
<s>the risks for our economy of a disorderly collapse of the euro are huge</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the comments from king and osborne come as investors nervously await the results of greek elections on sunday</s>
<s>rumors of possible coordinated actions by central banks around the globe to boost liquidity if needed lifted stocks thursday</s>
</p>
