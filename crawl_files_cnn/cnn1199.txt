<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  in early march hackers stole roughly 15 million credit card numbers from payment processor global payments a company that processes card transactions for companies like visa and mastercard</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fortunately the thieves only swiped card numbers and not cardholders names addresses or social security numbers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if hackers ever steal your information heres what you can do to protect your accounts according to lynnette khalfanicox a personal finance expert and author of perfect credit</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>contact the credit reporting agencies</s>
<s>experian equifax and transunion can put a fraud alert on your credit report so no one else can open other accounts in your name</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>file a police report</s>
<s>its a key document that serves as proof if say your wallet was stolen with credit cards or social security numbers inside</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>consider signing up for a credit monitoring service</s>
<s>often a company whose security has been breached will offer credit monitoring to affected cardholders for free</s>
<s>they will actively monitor your credit reports with the three major bureaus for a period of time after your information has been stolen</s>
<s>you will be alerted if anything suspicious comes up like changes made to your account information credit inquiries or a new account opened in your name</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for online purchases try a singleuse or virtual credit card</s>
<s>this option offered by banks like citibank discover and bank of america offers a different number than your main credit card</s>
<s>that way if hackers steal your account information from the retailer they dont have the number for your main credit card</s>
<s>you can also set a limit for how much can be charged against the virtual card</s>
