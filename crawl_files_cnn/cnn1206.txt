<p>
<s>you may have to look around a bit for a bargain stock to invest in but you should be able to find one</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  stocks have been sinking since april so youd think by now there would be an abundance of tempting shares to pick up on the cheap</s>
<s>alas youd be disappointed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unlike in the financial crisis when stock valuations sank to a 20year low real bargains are surprisingly hard to come by these days</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>thats because this market has felt a whole lot scarier than its actually been</s>
<s>the seemingly endless string of 4 daily swings you see on wall street masks the fact that equities are only now approaching bear market territory defined as a 20 drop in price</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in early october the sp 500 closed as low as 19 off its april highs</s>
<s>that sounds bad but stocks fell 57 during the financial panic</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>true some parts of the market  financial stocks in particular  are selling at going out of business prices</s>
<s>theres a reason for that though</s>
<s>with their fates clouded by debt fears in europe and proposed reforms in the us many financial shares pose a big risk to your portfolio especially if this faltering economy slips into a recession</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>theres an extraordinary number of wild cards and value traps out there says frank barbera comanager of the sierra core retirement fund</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mindful of these risks money interviewed more than a dozen money managers and strategists to find not just fallen stocks but discounted parts of the market offering real value with an added sense of security</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>should this recovery go into reverse the investments discussed below are unlikely to be hardest hit</s>
<s>assuming the economy can find another gear theyll go along for the ride</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>technology companies a source of cheap growth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>tech may not be the first sector that comes to mind when you think of cheap pockets of the market</s>
<s>after all these stocks are down just 4</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but right now tech is the cheapest of the growthoriented sectors says sp capital iq global equities strategist alec young</s>
<s>at a priceearnings ratio of 133 based on the past 12 months of earnings tech is 55 below its historic price tag</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>based on analysts forecast earnings which are starting to tick down marketwide techs projected pe of 118 is about on par with the broad market even though the sector typically trades at a near15 premium</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how is that possible</s>
<s>tech firms have seen profits grow much faster than the rest of the market in 2011  and they should enjoy betterthanaverage growth in the long term says kevin landis of the firsthand funds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a decade ago that faster growth was rewarded with skyhigh pes</s>
<s>but the model from the late 90s just doesnt hold anymore says mark schultz a fund manager with mtb investment advisors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>wont tech earnings which are keeping pes low be decimated if the economy sours</s>
<s>not necessarily</s>
<s>in the past five recessions the median tech companys earnings declined 10 compared with a 21 drop for the typical financial firm</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>you can get the broadest geographical exposure through ishares global technology sector index ixn which sprinkles in foreign holdings such as samsung and sap alongside the usual domestic suspects such as apple and microsoft</s>
<s>to stay inside the us try technology select sector spdr xlk which owns all the tech stocks in the sp 500</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>when it comes to individual stocks play it safe</s>
<s>intel intc fortune 500 has been paying and raising dividends for years and now sports a yield of 39</s>
<s>the stock trades at a projected pe of 9  thats more than a 20 discount to its peers</s>
<s>intel is still tagged an oldfashioned pc stock when in fact it stands to benefit from the tablet and smart phone revolution</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>more mobile devices in circulation means more servers are required and intel dominates the chip market for commercial servers</s>
<s>investors have underestimated intels growth potential says wells fargo analyst david wong</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>bluechip multinationals an emergingmarkets play</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the classic argument for investing in big industryleading businesses with a global reach used to be that they were safe bets</s>
<s>the new take they offer a cheaper way to gain exposure to the highflying emerging markets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>already half of all revenue generated by sp 500 firms comes from abroad and a growing percentage of that haul is pulled in from fastgrowing markets such as china india and latin america</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>due to their stability large stocks normally trade at a premium</s>
<s>at the end of 2008 the sps projected pe was 40 higher than the msci emerging markets indexs</s>
<s>since then emergingmarket shares have gained twice as much as the sp which is now at just a 20 premium</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the easiest and cheapest way to build a diversified portfolio of multinationals is through exchangetraded funds</s>
<s>usbased multinationals can be found through vanguard mega cap 300 mgc which invests in the 300 largest stocks in the domestic market</s>
<s>for multinationals with headquarters around the world theres ishares sp global 100 ioo</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with individual stocks the bigger the better</s>
<s>take energy giant chevron cvx fortune 500</s>
<s>at a projected pe of 67 its stock is 14 cheaper than rival exxon mobil xom fortune 500s partly because of concerns that chevron isnt expanding its energy reserves fast enough</s>
<s>forester capital management analyst jason mcrae says those fears are overblown noting chevrons recent discovery in the gulf of mexico</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>there are plenty of risks in betting on energy in a weak economy</s>
<s>but chevron is wellpositioned to sell into fastgrowing asian and latin american markets</s>
<s>the firm also has a lower cost structure than its competitors which makes it a safer bet when oil prices fall</s>
<s>during the 200709 recession chevron shares fell just 25 vs a 34 decline for the sector and a 38 drop for the sp 500</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>medical equipment makers flying under the radar</s>
</p>

<p>

</p>

<p>
<s>other types of equipment makers though are being overlooked</s>
<s>covidien cov sells supplies and devices for everything from basic medical care to major surgeries</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>though it faces a possible new medical device tax in 2013 the eventual increase in patient volume thanks to expanding coverage should boost sales to offset it says mark finn who runs the t rowe price value fund</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at a pe of 105 the stock trades at a near20 discount to its medical equipment peers</s>
<s>covidien doubled its rd spending in recent years and has been restructuring its lineup  efforts that temporarily weighed on profits but should eventually pay off</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>baxter bax fortune 500 also trades at a discount thanks in part to the threat of new competition in the hemophilia market</s>
<s>but baxter is making inroads in other areas like hometherapy devices for dialysis patients to go along with its highly profitable vaccines and biopharmaceuticals</s>
</p>
