<p>
<s>spanish bond yields continue to rise after greek elections</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  as the political crisis in greece slowly moves toward a resolution spain continues to lose credibility in the bond market</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in a sign of continued jitters over the euro debt crisis the yield on spanish 10year bonds jumped to 71 up from about 69 late last week</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the yield which rises when prices fall first rose above 7 last week marking the highest level since the euro was launched in 1999</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spain cannot afford to borrow at 7 for long and analysts say the nation is effectively shut out of the market at these levels</s>
<s>in addition the 7 level is psychologically important because investors see it as a potential trigger for a government bailout</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rise in spanish bond yields comes as investors digest news out of greece</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new democracy one of the political parties that signed off on greeces bailout earlier this year won the most votes in sundays election</s>
<s>but the outlook for greece remains uncertain as politicians must now form a coalition government something they failed to do last month</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the concern is that instability in greece could further undermine confidence in the spanish bond market</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the solvency risk in spain and its banking sector is likely to dominate markets as the smoke on the greek elections clear said fixedincome analysts at nomura securities in a note to clients</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spanish bond yields have surged as investors worry the nation might need to be bailed out</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the spanish government has requested up to €100 billion from the european union to recapitalize insolvent banks</s>
<s>but the rescue plan failed to ease concerns about the sustainability of spains public debt</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>moodys and fitch both downgraded spains credit rating following the request for aid which many analysts say simply transfers the banks bad debt to the government</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spain is expected to announce the results of an independent bank audit later this week which will determine exactly how much the government needs to borrow</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in addition spain is set to auction shortterm notes on thursday</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spanish banks have been the main buyers of spanish debt earlier this year but banks appear to have slowed their purchases in recent weeks noted the nomura analysts </s>
</p>
