<s>manufacturing slowed in june contracting for the first time in nearly 3 years ism says</s>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  us manufacturing slowed sharply in june to a level that indicates the sector is now contracting marking the end of almost three years of growth in domestic manufacturing</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the institute for supply management reported monday that its monthly index fell to 497 in june from a 535 reading in may</s>
<s>any reading below 50 indicates contraction in the sector</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the report is a worrying sign for the economy</s>
<s>manufacturing had been something of a bright spot during the recovery and the sector had expanded for 34 months in a row before june</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the index came in below the expectations of economists surveyed by briefingcom who forecast it would fall to 522</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the indexs new orders component a forwardlooking indicator was particularly hard hit</s>
<s>it fell 123 percentage points in june to 478  the first contraction in new orders since april 2009</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>sam bullard a senior economist at wells fargo said that manufacturers are reacting to significant global headwinds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the deepening of the eurozone recession and the slowing of chinas economy is really having an impact on manufacturers bullard said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>investors reacted negatively to the new manufacturing numbers and all three major indexes dropped sharply in the minutes after the reports release</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the report is likely to feed worries in the us over slower growth in the second half of the year as reports on both economic growth and the labor market have been lackluster in recent months</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>paul dales senior us economist at capital economics said in a research note that a reading of 497 does not mean the economy is heading toward a recession</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a reading of below 470 is required to be consistent with another recession dales said</s>
<s>this means the index is still consistent with a growing economy albeit at an annualized rate of a little below 1</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>investors will get another important data point on friday when the government is due to release its latest jobs report</s>
<s>economists expect only around 100000 jobs to be created  but anything below that number will set off alarm bells</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the us isnt the only country experiencing a slowdown in manufacturing</s>
<s>china reported sunday that its oncebooming manufacturing sector is now growing at an extremely slow rate</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the china manufacturing purchasing managers index for june fell to 502 from 504 in may</s>
<s>just like the us gauge any reading below 50 signals contraction in the manufacturing sector</s>
</p>
