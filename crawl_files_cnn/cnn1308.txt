<p>
<s>tax prep never gets any easier</s>
<s>to save the most you can this april  and in years to come  you need to master these key challenges</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  with the bush tax cuts slated to expire at the end of this election year consider this filing season the calm before the tax storm youll face few new rules tax rates are the same as last year and popular deductions are still in place</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but preparing your income taxes remains as tricky as ever</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>heres how to make sure you dont get tripped up by five common filing challenges  and how to set yourself up for tax savings on next years return and beyond</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>filing challenge when it comes to paying taxes on investment gains whats new this year is a key reporting rule for any stocks you bought on or after jan 1 2011 you no longer get to set your cost basis thats the cost of your investment for tax purposes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your broker will do it for you based on the accounting method you chose  your broker should have sent you a notice  or the firms default whats called firstin firstout or fifo which assumes you sold your oldest shares</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>check 1099s against your records and have your broker fix any errors</s>
<s>well see a lot of revisions on the 1099s says roman ciosek a partner at hightowers strata wealth management whos advising clients not to rush to file</s>
<s>you cant however change your costbasis method once youve sold  no matter whether it was your choice or your brokers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>otherwise the rules are the same</s>
<s>you can offset your capital gains with capital losses new and lingering</s>
<s>first match longterm gains on assets held more than one year and taxed at 15 with longterm losses and shortterm gains held one year or less and taxed as ordinary income with shortterm losses</s>
<s>then match longterm against shortterm</s>
<s>if you have extra losses you can deduct up to $3000 from your income and roll over the rest to trim your taxes next year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how to plan going forward you can switch methods any time and options include lastin firstout and specificshare identification</s>
<s>whats best depends on many factors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if youve invested in a stock over time and have your biggest gains on the first batch you got for instance you might want to identify specific shares to sell rather than use fifo</s>
<s>but if you have ample capital losses to offset gains this might be a good year to reap big profits</s>
<s>frequent traders or anyone who has bought a lot of a stock over time can run the numbers using netbasis software netbasiscom $20 and up</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rules extend to mutual funds most exchangetraded funds and dividendreinvestment plans in 2012 then bonds in 2013</s>
<s>chances are your fund companies have already written to ask what method you want to use the typical default is average cost</s>
<s>dont ignore this paperwork</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>filing challenge you have until tax day to fund a traditional or roth ira for 2011 april 17 this year assuming you can</s>
<s>with a traditional deductible ira your contributions are in pretax dollars and your withdrawals are taxable</s>
<s>with a roth you pay the taxes upfront but neither you nor your heirs will owe income taxes on withdrawals</s>
<s>for most the roth has the edge over the long term</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>since 2010 everyone has been able to convert a traditional ira to a roth regardless of income</s>
<s>thats great news  unless it means an unexpectedly high tax bill</s>
<s>this year you owe taxes on 2011 conversions</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>plus if you converted in 2010 and spread your tax payments over two years  a special break for 2010 only  you owe half those taxes now</s>
<s>in a real pinch</s>
<s>you have until filing day to undo a 2011 conversion</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how to plan you can open an ira for 2012 now too as well as move an old ira into a roth</s>
<s>converting is especially advantageous if youll face a higher tax rate in retirement than you do now which is hard to predict</s>
<s>but for anyone considering it this is a good year to pull the trigger</s>
<s>you know youll pay no more than 35 on the rollover while the expiration of the bush tax cuts could usher in higher rates</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>filing challenge when you hit the jackpot with your home you owe capital gains taxes on any profits above $500000 for marrieds $250000 for singles</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>what if like many homeowners today you sell at a loss</s>
<s>youre out of luck</s>
<s>you cant take a deduction for the hit you took on your primary residence</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>you may be entitled though to a tax break if your mortgage was reduced through a restructuring the bank agreed to a short sale or you lost your home to foreclosure</s>
<s>typically this kind of relief is considered income since you no longer have to repay the debt</s>
<s>under a tax break put in place during the real estate crisis you can exclude up to $2 million in forgiven debt from your income</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how to plan this exclusion expires at the end of the year</s>
<s>if you need a break dont wait to act</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>filing challenge sorting through education tax perks isnt easy</s>
<s>valuable options include the tuition and fees deduction $4000 max the lifetime learning credit $2000 per return and the american opportunity credit $2500 per student for undergraduate work only but you can take only one per student in a single tax year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in general a credit is more valuable than a deduction since it directly cuts your taxes while a deduction merely trims how much income is taxed</s>
<s>in the 28 tax bracket a $2000 credit trumps a $4000 deduction which lowers your federal tax bill by just $1120</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if you qualify for the american opportunity credit thats the biggest bang for your buck says justin ransome a partner in grant thorntons national tax office</s>
<s>income limits apply to all but the aocs is the highest adjusted gross income of $180000 for married couples $90000 for singles</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how to plan your choice may be simpler next year the tuition deduction expired at the end of 2011</s>
<s>the american opportunity credit is due to run out this year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>filing challenge the bar for deducting health care costs is high  you can write off only those expenses that exceed 75 of your agi</s>
<s>but rising health care costs may bring it within reach</s>
<s>if your medical expenses went up significantly or your income was reduced dramatically pull out the shoebox of receipts says allison shipley a principal at pricewaterhousecoopers personal financial services practice</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the definition of allowable expenses is broad doctors and dentists bills prescriptions glasses hearing aids wheelchairs transportation to doctors appointments nursinghome fees and certain insurance costs medicare b and d but not part a for example</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>how to plan the threshold jumps to 10 of your agi in 2013 so consider moving up some medical expenses this year if you think youll be close to qualifying in 2012</s>
</p>
