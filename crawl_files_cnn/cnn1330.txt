<p>
<s>as the west deals with the overhang of too much debt and a potential fiscal cliff in the united states some of the economies of asia have remained quite resilient</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>malaysia thailand and indonesia have continued to report economic growth above imf projections and also have relatively low inflation rates</s>
<s>china despite a slowing economy is still growing at a pace that is the envy of the developed world</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the asian growth story has not been derailed</s>
<s>and even though there may be a global slowdown in 2013 there are several companies in asia wellpositioned for strong growth over the next few years</s>

<p>
<s>these five represent a mix of economies and sectors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>tencent tencent is one of chinas largest internet companies providing services from microblogging qqcom to a handful of online games</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>i love their acquisition of riot games whose immensely popular league of legends has more than 33 million players worldwide</s>
<s>tencent has also purchased stakes in other gaming distribution companies serving the philippines singapore and brazil and has secured china licensing deals with zynga znga and electronic arts eaowned popcap as well</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this is a growth play on the burgeoning middle class and youthful demographics around asia</s>
<s>in tencents most recent quarter sales surged 80 from a year ago</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the stock which trades in hong kong under the ticker symbol 0700 and has a small number of shares listed in new york is not cheap</s>
<s>it trades at nearly 30 times 2012 earnings estimates</s>
<s>but tencent tcehymay be worth it as long as it continues to grow so rapidly</s>
<s>i like tencent more than other chinese online media firms like baidu bidu and sina sina because of tencents ability to generate fees from games as opposed to being so dependent on mobile ad revenue</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>keppel corp keppel based in singapore is the largest builder of offshore oil rigs in the world</s>

<p>

<p>
<s>cache logistics trust this real estate investment trust invests in warehouses around singapore</s>
<s>the stock trades under the symbol cache in singapore and there is no listing in the us</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>i am bullish in general about the economies of southeast asia</s>
<s>singapore reits have done particularly well in the past 18 months  especially ones focused on office and commercial property</s>

<p>
<s>singapores status as a commodities trading hub will only increase with asias rise as a manufacturing and consumption powerhouse</s>

<p>
<s>advanced info services ais is the largest telecom company in thailand</s>
<s>singapore telecom the regions largest carrier has a 23 stake in ais</s>
<s>but i like ais over singtel because of its improving fundamentals</s>
<s>ais has pushed net margins up from 148 in 2008 to 176 in 2011 while singtels margins have contracted from 231 to 212 in the same time period</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ais is a solid way to invest in the continued growth of thailands economy where a larger middle class is emerging</s>
<s>ais is a more pure play on asian telecom growth without the baggage of singtels presence across multiple markets</s>
</p>
