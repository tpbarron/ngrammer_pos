<s>hong kong cnnmoney  the obama administration filed a complaint monday with the world trade organization alleging that china has illegally subsidized automotive exports and undercut american suppliers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the complaint accuses china of providing $1 billion in illegal subsidies to auto and auto parts exporters between 2009 and 2011 according to a senior administration official</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the obama administration says that the benefits provided to chinese companies violate a wto prohibition on exportcontingent subsidies</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in response china filed its own case against the united states on monday according to the wto</s>
<s>details of the complaint were not immediately available</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the wto is the global organization that referees trade disputes between nations</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the chinese auto industry has grown rapidly in the last decade as chinas growing middle class made the nation the largest market for car purchases in the world</s>
<s>general motors gm fortune 500 now sells more cars in china than in the united states</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>chinese exporters have managed in recent years to quickly ramp up their shipments of auto parts to the worlds largest economy according to the obama administration complaint</s>
<s>the white house estimates that up to 60 of chinas auto parts exporters benefit from subsidies making it hard for american companies to compete</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>china exports virtually no assembled cars to the united states but the export of auto parts by china is having a big impact on us jobs</s>
<s>the parts industry in the united states employs far more workers than the automakers who assemble and sell the finished cars</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in a related case filed in july the administration launched a complaint against china with the wto over $3 billion in duties beijing placed on us auto exports</s>
</p>

<s>conversely us industries face increasing competition from imports forcing the weaker companies out of business</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the us trade gap with china the nations most important  and controversial  trading partner widened to a record $280 billion last year and is on pace to get even bigger this year</s>

<p>

<p>

<s>this includes getting beijing to let its currency the yuan rise in comparison to other currencies rather than keeping it pegged to the dollar</s>

<p>
