<p>
<s>in 2012 aim to save more but dont rule out borrowing or applying for rewards cards</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  lets face it 2011 was pretty abysmal when it came to savings rates and 2012 wont be much brighter</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the average savings account barely pays 02 while moneymarket funds havent surpassed 002 since march</s>
<s>with the federal reserve promising to sit on shortterm interest rates until at least 2013 dont expect any help from uncle sam when it comes to saving</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that means in the coming year it will be up to you to eke out what little you can in savings rates  and to preserve those yields from rising bank fees</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while households in general have become smarter about managing their debt the average card balance dropped by $500 to about $7000 in 2011  savings dont look particularly healthy says richard barrington of moneyratescom</s>
<s>the personal savings rate isnt near its 2007 lows but it dipped to 36 in september</s>
<s>and the pattern of recent months suggests it will continue in that direction next year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the action plan  save wisely borrow judiciously and take what the market is giving you</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>search for yield</s>
<s>online banks with less overhead are a great place to start</s>
<s>savings accounts at american express axp fortune 500 or discover bank dfs fortune 500 yield 1  no great shakes but five times the national average</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>also check out highyielding checking options at a local bank where you can often find rates above 2</s>
<s>there are tradeoffs though</s>
<s>for instance to get that rate you may have to commit to making at least 10 debit card transactions a month and sign up for direct deposit</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>search for the best rates where you live at moneyratescomrewardscheckinghtm</s>
<s>call the bank about terms and qualifications that may apply</s>
<s>also visit bankratecom to make sure the banks youre considering earn at least three stars for safety</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>dont rule out borrowing</s>
<s>expect to see aggressive offers from banks whether its for a mortgage or a credit card says sherief meleis a partner at novantas</s>
<s>in november for example the average homeequity loan was charging 698 and credit card aprs went as low as 7</s>
<s>be advised though lending standards arent loosening</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>apply for a rewards card</s>
<s>your excellent credit score qualifies you for the crèmedelacrème of offers</s>
<s>signup bonuses are up 25  and are expected to stay that way for the coming year according to nerdwalletcom which tracks credit card offers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>dont wait too long to apply with debit card reform passed legislators are likely to turn to credit cards next</s>
<s>and who knows how that will affect your perks </s>
</p>
