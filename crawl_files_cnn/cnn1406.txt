<s>it takes about 66 days for repeated behaviors to forge a good habit</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  regular savings is one of the surest ways to increase your net worth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this is part of a special report on 101 ways to build wealth</s>
<s>in this story readers and experts weigh in with advice for significantly boosting your savings</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>stick to a budget for just a month</s>
<s>a canadian study found that people who stuck to a budget had higher net worth than those who didnt have one or had one they didnt follow</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>know you cant commit to a lifetime of constraint</s>
<s>financial planner sean dowling of stamford conn suggests focusing on your biggest spending problem area and tracking it with quicken for just a single month</s>
<s>then set an attainable goal for the following month  like one latte out for every two drip coffees at home</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>then bump up savings for the next three</s>
<s>studies show that it takes about 66 days for repeated behaviors to become habits</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so tell yourself youre going to try to bank your latte savings for three months or so to see if you can manage without the cash leaving yourself the option to reevaluate once the trial run is over</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>most people find that their budget naturally adjusts says t rowe price investment services financial planner stuart ritter</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>restore equilibrium regularly</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a vanguard study spanning 1989 to 2009 showed that regularly rebalancing a portfolio to target stock and bond allocations increased returns on average by 05</s>
<s>to get similar results rebalance when your target allocation strays more than 10</s>
<s>the reason it works is classic youre essentially buying low and selling high</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hammer the message home</s>
<s>a 2010 study showed that people who received monthly text messages from their bank about their savings goals put away 6 more than those who didnt</s>
<s>for savings above what youve automated set up alerts on your phone</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>find a carrot  or a stick</s>
<s>dean karlan an econ professor at yale has found that commitment contracts help people stay on track to reach their savings goals</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so enlist a buddy to hold you accountable and allow yourself rewards say a massage for successes along the way</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>or if the carrot approach doesnt do it for you use stickkcom</s>
<s>you input your goals and authorize the site to charge a certain amount to your credit card  then send it to a person or organization of your choice like a political candidate you hate  if you fail to hit your target</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>put away your raises</s>
<s>each time you get a bump up in your salary dedicate a portion of that extra pay  say half  to increasing retirement contributions or other savings</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if a 35yearold starts out saving 6 of his income for retirement and then puts half of his 3ayear raises toward his 401k for the next five years hed be at a 15 annual contribution rate at the end of that period assuming he also gets a 3 company match</s>
<s>that rate would allow the saver to maintain his lifestyle over a 30year retirement  even if he went on shopping sprees with all his subsequent raises</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>autoescalate your 401k</s>
<s>dont count on your good intentions to put that raise away</s>
<s>some 40 of plans let participants set retirement contributions to increase annually reports aon hewitt take advantage</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money readers weigh in pay the mortgage even after its erased</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>when we paid off our home i opened a new savings account and every month since i have deposited the same amount into it that i used to use to pay the mortgage</s>
<s>now we have $20000 in the account</s>
<s>  nona cervenka chicago</s>
</p>
