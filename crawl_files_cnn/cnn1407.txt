<p>
<s>the race isnt always easy</s>

</p>

<p>
<s>what you need is a longterm strategy that will help you stay the course and finish strong</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that starts with a diversified core of investments</s>
<s>from there as you age youll make shifts that take into account your changing financial stamina</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>go for major growth</s>
<s>by your midthirties youve likely accumulated some real money</s>
<s>time to start getting serious about how you invest those savings</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in this first stage you want to build in lots of potential for growth</s>
<s>so 70 to 80 of your money should be in stocks divvied among us companies large and small and foreign companies in both emerging and developed markets</s>
<s>index funds are the name of the game</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>theyll give you the broadest foundation says fran kinniry a principal in vanguards investment strategy group</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the following money 70 index funds will provide ample diversification and growth potential for your early years</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>us stocks put 43 into large caps 12 into small caps</s>
<s>schwab sp 500 index swppx and vanguard smallcap index naesx today its possible to invest in one index fund or etf and get exposure to nearly every us stock</s>
<s>but youll lose control over how much money goes into small companies which have potential for higher growth</s>
<s>to be more deliberate go with separate large and smallcap funds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>international stocks 18</s>
<s>fidelity spartan international index fsiix and vanguard emerging markets stock index veiex these two funds invest in developed and emerging markets respectively</s>
<s>since foreign stocks make up roughly half the world equity market cap they shouldnt be ignored</s>
<s>still planners advise against keeping more than 30 of your stock slice abroad since such equities have drawbacks including currency risk</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>us bonds 15</s>
<s>vanguard total bond market index vbmfx because you dont yet have a big fixedincome stake you want a fund with wide exposure to highquality us bonds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>alternatives 5</s>
<s>cohen  steers realty shares csrsx real estate doesnt usually move in lockstep with stocks or bonds</s>
<s>thus reits can diversify your mix</s>
<s>the group has gotten pricey though so rather than an index go for cohen  steers the funds managers have a good record of finding value</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>over the past 20 years stocks of midsize companies  those with market values of $2 billion to $18 billion  have returned 11 annually trumping large 8 and small firms 9</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>you already own some midcaps via your us stock funds but a bigger share may give you more growth  though with extra risk since midcaps are more volatile than large companies</s>
</p>

<p>
