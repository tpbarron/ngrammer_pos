<p>
<s>the senates permanent subcommittee on investigations said londonbased hsbc hbc failed to review thousands of suspicious transactions and properly vet clients over the past decade</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>among other issues the report notes that in 2007 and 2008 hsbcs mexico unit shipped $7 billion in cash to the banks us affiliate a volume of shipments that law enforcement officials said could reach that size only if they included illegal drug proceeds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hsbc mexico had a number of highprofile clients linked to drug trafficking the report says as well as a huge backlog of accounts marked for closure due to suspicious activity but whose closures were delayed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the report also found that hsbc worked extensively with saudi arabias al rajhi bank some owners of which have been linked to terrorism financing according to a cia report quoted by the subcommittee</s>
<s>some evidence suggests al rajhis key founder was an early financial benefactor of al qaeda the report says</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hsbcs us affiliate supplied al rajhi with nearly $1 billion worth of us banknotes up to 2010 and also worked with two banks in bangladesh that some evidence links to terrorism financing as well</s>

<p>
<s>from an oversight perspective the failure of accountability here is dramatic said sen carl levin chairman of the subcommittee</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the department of justice is also investigating hsbc over the issue</s>
<s>a doj spokeswoman declined to comment citing the ongoing probe</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the report also said hsbcs us affiliate handled nearly 25000 transactions involving iran between 2001 and 2007 despite us sanctions against the country</s>
<s>other hsbc affiliates making transfers to the us frequently stripped information from the transactions that linked them to iran in order to evade scrutiny</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>some hsbc executives in the us were aware of this practice as far back as 2001 the report says</s>
<s>an outside review commissioned by hsbc found nearly $20 billion worth of transactions between 2001 and 2007 that may have been subject to us sanctions</s>

<p>
<s>the report came ahead of a hearing by the senate subcommittee tuesday that featured testimony from hsbc executives and government officials from the treasury department the department of homeland security and the office of the comptroller of the currency</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>regulators also came in for criticism in the report  in particular the occ hsbcs primary overseer</s>

<p>

<p>

</p>

<p>

<s>we believe that this case history will provide important lessons for the whole industry in seeking to prevent illicit actors entering the global financial system</s>

<p>
<s>the bank added that it has beefed up its compliance efforts over the past year increasing its due diligence requirements for affiliates and devoting more resources to the issue</s>

<p>
