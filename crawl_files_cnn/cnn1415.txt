<s>hong kong cnnmoney  manufacturing growth in china continued to slide in september according to a key initial reading released thursday</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hsbcs initial purchasing managers index for chinese manufacturing ticked up slightly to 478 in september from 476 the bank said thursday</s>
<s>any reading below 50 indicates that factory growth is shrinking rather than picking up speed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>chinas manufacturing growth is still slowing but the pace of slowdown is stabilizing hongbin qu an economist at hsbc said in a statement</s>
<s>this is adding more pressures to the labor market and has prompted beijing to step up easing over the past weeks</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>manufacturing in china is considered a barometer of the global economy because of the countrys role as a powerhouse exporter</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>china the worlds secondlargest economy behind the united states has been hit particularly hard by the recession in much of europe</s>
<s>the european sovereign debt crisis has prompted steep austerity measures in many countries</s>
<s>weak conditions have zapped demand in the eurozone the largest market for chinese exports</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in addition the us economy has slowed further cutting demand for chinese exports</s>
<s>the slowdown in china also worries investors because china has become a major market for us companies</s>
</p>

<p>

<s>and goldman sachs has issued a slightly less dour outlook for china growth  dropping it to 76 from 80</s>
</p>

<p>

<s>the countrys central bank cut rates in june and july  the first such actions since 2008</s>
