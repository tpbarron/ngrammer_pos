<p>
<s>getting a stay in rehab covered by medicare after a long observation stay in a hospital isnt easy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  when you need to go to rehab after a fall illness or other medical problem medicare will fully cover the cost of your stay for up to 20 days as long as youve spent at least three days in the hospital as an inpatient among other criteria</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as discussed in our companion article the painful new trend in medicare an increasing number of seniors are spending days in the hospital under observation only</s>
<s>the result your subsequent care at a skilled nursing facility doesnt qualify for medicare coverage</s>
<s>you could be stuck with the full tab</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>following these steps during and after an observation stay however can help you avoid a devastating bill</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your best shot at getting medicare to cover a skilled nursing stay is to have your status switched to inpatient while youre in the hospital says terry berthelot an attorney at the center for medicare advocacy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>dont assume anything</s>
<s>ask your doctor and case manager what your or your parents designation is</s>
<s>if the answer is observation press the doctor to review your status and take your case to the utilization review committee</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>make sure your full medical history including past illnesses is noted in your records</s>
<s>a more complete picture of your health could help you meet inpatient guidelines</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>bring in help</s>
<s>ask your primary physician to call the hospitalist and explain what risk factors or conditions warrant a higher level of care</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>arrange home care</s>
<s>if all else fails and you cant afford to go to a nursing facility talk to the discharge planner</s>
<s>medicare covers a limited amount of home help even if you werent an inpatient</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>once youve been discharged the hospital cant switch your status</s>
<s>your best hope is to appeal your case to medicare  a tough process</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>lay the groundwork</s>
<s>ask the nursing home to bill medicare  thats not automatic if youre not admitted directly from an inpatient stay</s>
<s>medicare will then formally deny the stay</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>be patient</s>
<s>you cant start the appeals process until you receive your quarterly medicare summary notice</s>
<s>make a copy circle the skilled nursing facility charge and write that you are appealing the denial on the grounds that your hospital stay should have been considered inpatient not observation</s>
<s>mail it to the address provided on the statement</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>expect to lose</s>
<s>once medicare has denied your appeal twice  thats common says berthelot  your case moves to an administrative law judge</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the hearing may be by phone but you can request a video conference and medicare must cover those costs</s>
<s>this is where advocates report some success though expect to work for it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ask a doctor to testify on your behalf or write a letter and have the nursing home testify that your care met medicare guidelines</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>find help</s>
<s>for guidance contact the nonprofit center for medicare advocacy at medicareadvocacyorg</s>
</p>
