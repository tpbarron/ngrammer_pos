<p>
<s>stock markets have done better than the doubters expected</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  had you looked back in january to see how stocks did in the prior decade youd have been underwhelmed by annualized gains of just 3</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>repeat that exercise today and youll get a decidedly brighter picture the dow has gained nearly 9 a year and the sp 500 more than 8 now that the effects of the march 2000 to october 2002 bear market are more than a decade in the rearview mirror</s>
<s>thats nearing the 98 historical average return of us stocks</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>no this doesnt erase the sting of the bursting of the tech bubble or the 2008 crash nor does it mean you should put on rosecolored glasses as you read about 8 unemployment and a looming fiscal cliff</s>
<s>nevertheless the lost decade for stocks is no more</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so if the past wasnt quite so bad you ought to at least question the currently fashionable position that the future is really going to stink</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as governments worldwide struggle to pay down debt the theory goes economic activity and stock returns will be depressed for years</s>
<s>this is what bond guru bill gross and his colleagues at pimco call the new normal</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ben inker head of asset allocation at gmo points out a problem with this assumption</s>
<s>we dont disagree when it comes to expectations for economic growth says inker</s>
<s>where we disagree is whether the new normal has to have a bad impact on stock returns</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>research by a team at the london business school showing no relationship between how fast economies grow and how well stocks perform supports his view</s>
<s>case in point since 1985 the us economy grew less than a third as fast as chinas yet american stocks gained more than twice as much as chinese shares</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related china handle with caution</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>inkers firm forecasts that over the next seven years highquality us stocks should produce real or inflationadjusted returns of 44 a year while foreign shares return 5 based on factors including priceearning ratios</s>
<s>again thats close to though still less than longterm averages</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>duncan richardson chief equity investment officer at eaton vance argues the us is the most attractive major market when it comes to the fundamentals</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>whats more sentiment as measured by money flowing into or actually out of stock funds is as bad as its been in more than two decades</s>
<s>most analysts believe thats really a bullish sign</s>
</p>

</p>

<p>
<s>this doesnt mean its all smooth sailing ahead</s>
<s>europes recession and chinas decelerating growth rate are cutting into us corporate earnings this year</s>
<s>profit growth for sp 500 companies is expected to reaccelerate in 2013 but that assumes things dont get worse from here</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so yes the risks are real but the doomandgloom forecasts feel a bit overwrought</s>
</p>
