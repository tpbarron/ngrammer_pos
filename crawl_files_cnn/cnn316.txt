<s>postal service takes another step toward insolvency</s>

<p>

<s>but it wont do it because it doesnt have the money</s>
<s>it will be the second time that the service has defaulted on a payment the first occurring on aug 1</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for the time being the default means little</s>
<s>this default will have no effect on the processing or delivery of mail and employees and suppliers will continue to be paid said david partenheimer a spokesman for the postal service</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but if congress does nothing come next spring the postal service will truly start running out of cash</s>
<s>that means the agency may not have enough to pay mail carriers and subcontractors which could mean drastic cuts to the mail delivery system  postal service armageddon</s>

<s>the financial crunch is due to a law passed in 2006 that only congress can fix</s>

<s>neither of the bills moving through congress completely undo that mandate  they just prolong or delay it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but unions in particular blame congress and the prefunding mandate for the default which they call bogus and manufactured</s>
<s> they want congress to repeal the mandate</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this default is not a crisis</s>
<s>the postal service already has set aside $45 billion for future retiree health benefits  more than any other organization in america and enough to pay for decades of future retiree healthcare said national association of letter carriers president fredric rolando</s>
<s>the payment in question results from an unnecessary congressional mandate  an obligation not required of any other company or agency in the country</s>
</p>

<p>

</p>

<p>

<p>

<s>but the house has yet to consider a measure drafted by rep darrell issa a california republican that has only gone through a committee and greatly differs from the senate version</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>come sept 30 the postal service will be forced to default again  further eroding confidence in its future and in congress ability to provide it with the reforms it needs to save itself said tom carper a delaware democrat who is one of the authors of the senate bill</s>
