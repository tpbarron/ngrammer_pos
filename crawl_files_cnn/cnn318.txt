<p>
<s>a total of nearly 2 billion facebook shares could come to market in the next nine months as lockup rules expire</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  facebooks postipo life has been rocky and on thursday it will face one of the toughest times for a newly public company the first day that some insiders are allowed to dump the stock</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>like many initial public offerings facebooks may 18 debut included a lockup agreement that requires some shareholders to hold on to their stock for a certain period</s>
<s>when a companys lockedup shares are set free the stock typically tanks since millions of new shares flood in at once</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebooks first reckoning day is here</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>insiders will be free to sell off about 271 million shares on thursday</s>
<s>since facebook fb has lost nearly half its value since its debut some may jump at the chance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the reasoning behind lockups which usually last 90 to 180 days is to prevent the market from being swamped with too many of a companys shares immediately after an ipo</s>
<s>keeping stocks scarce can help boost their value</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebooks fellow newly public internet companies felt the pain when their lockups ended</s>
<s>linkedin lnkd slumped as much as 7 on its lockup expiration day and groupon grpn fell 10 to hit a new low</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>thursday is only the first in a series of lockup expirations for facebook</s>
<s>its unusual tiered system means several more waves are coming and a total of 18 billion shares could hit the market in the next nine months</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ken sena an analyst at evercore partners warned in a note to clients that the stock drag is likely to be drawn out because of the staggered schedule</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but sp capital iq analyst scott kessler shrugged off the concerns and even upgraded his rating on facebook to buy from hold on tuesday</s>
<s>we do not expect early employees and investors will be aggressive sellers of fb shares at current levels he wrote in a client note</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the big facebook stock dump could come in midnovember</s>
<s>thats when facebook will convert the special form of restricted stock units or rsus held by most of its staff into actual shares of its stock</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebooks employees will owe taxes this year on the value of that stock</s>
<s>like most companies that issue rsus facebook is handling the logistics on its employees behalf</s>
<s>the employees are still ultimately responsible for making sure their tax bills are fully covered</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebook plans to withhold a big chunk of its employees shares  roughly 120 million  and sell them on the open market to cover the tax bill</s>
<s>selling off so many shares at once could hammer the companys stock price</s>
<s>so facebook has a plan b it can tap its credit lines and cash reserves to pay off the tax bill without issuing equity</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a facebook representative declined to comment on which route the company plans to take</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebook has carefully lined up its options</s>
<s>it took out a $3 billion credit line specifically earmarked for the potential rsulinked tax bill and its currently sitting on $10 billion in cash</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at facebooks current share price the tax bill would total around $26 billion</s>
<s>if its stock price keeps dropping that bill will shrink further</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>facebook did get a vote of confidence from one investor last week</s>
<s>netflix nflx ceo reed hastings whos also a member of facebooks board disclosed to regulators that he recently bought $1 million worth of shares in the social network</s>
