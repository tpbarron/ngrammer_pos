<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  more than a week after minnesota shuttered all but essential government services theres no end in sight to the budget impasse</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>after a contentious series of meetings since the july 1 shutdown governor mark dayton and republican lawmakers have no talks scheduled for this weekend</s>
<s>instead the two sides are trading blame for the negotiations hitting the skids</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>sunday will mark minnesotas longest shutdown since 2005 as well as one of the longest of any state in the past 10 years</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>though critical public safety and health services continue most state agencies are either closed or operating with a skeletal crew</s>
<s>many businesses and residents cant get licenses or permits</s>
<s>state parks are closed</s>
<s>and some nonprofits that provide social services for the state are not getting paid</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on wednesday dayton proposed a mix of tax increases surcharges and payment deferrals to bridge the remaining $14 billion budget gap</s>
<s>he gave lawmakers a choice between temporarily adding a 2 incometax surcharge on millionaires or raising cigarette taxes by $1 a pack</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but republican leaders said any tax increase at all is a nonstarter</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it was very disappointing and a step backwards house speaker kurt zellers said of the governors proposal</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>even a solution put forth by a group of minnesota wisemen did not help ease the tension</s>
<s>the former politicians state officials and business leaders suggested thursday a mix of spending cuts and payment deferrals along with a temporary income tax surcharge of 4 on everyone and a hike in cigarette and alcohol levies</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>republicans rejected the proposal while dayton said he didnt agree with increasing taxes on all residents</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>my goal has consistently been to protect most minnesotans from either an income tax increase or a property tax increase by raising state income taxes on only the wealthiest 2 of minnesotans he said</s>
<s>most other minnesotans are already overtaxed due primarily to the 75 increase in property taxes statewide during the previous eight years</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while the standoff continues more bills are starting to get paid</s>
<s>the state courts have slowly been allowing more funding to flow to agencies and other groups</s>
<s>on wednesday a judge agreed to permit the state department of human services to reopen its division that licenses those providing critical services to children and vulnerable adults</s>
<s>and special education funding will continue to flow to the schools</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>meanwhile on friday fitch ratings downgraded minnesotas debt ratings to doubleaplus down from triple a because of the budget dispute</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the rating agency also highlighted minnesotas reliance on onetime budget fixes during the recession and the likelihood that any budget accord will contain more of them</s>
<s>fitch said the most significant of these measures were payment deferrals to school districts and the use of funds from the federal stimulus program</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on a positive note fitch praised minnesota for its balanced economy with a recovering job market</s>
<s>the agency also said the state has a strong debt position with a debt burden that is on the low end of the moderate range at about 27 of personal income</s>
</p>
