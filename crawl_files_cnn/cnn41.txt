<p>
<s>the us chamber of commerce lost big time this election</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>washington cnnmoney  most of the $36 million that the us chamber of commerce spent on its probusiness agenda this election went down the drain</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>according to federal records compiled by the center for responsive politics a watchdog group $29 million of the money was spent in races that the chambers favored candidate lost</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in 15 us senate races the chamber spent $24 million</s>
<s>only two went the groups way the victories of republican senators dean heller of nevada and orrin hatch of utah whose race wasnt very competitive</s>
<s>in seven races the chamber spent between $1 million and $4 million each to defeat democrats and lost them all</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the house the chamber did better but not much</s>
<s>of the $51 million spent to influence some 22 house races only five went their way</s>
<s>in 10 of those races the chamber spent between $300000 and $500000 opposing democrats  mostly from illinois california and new york  all of whom won</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for its part the chamber was mum on its 2012 election losses</s>

</p>

<p>
<s>donohue said the chamber aimed to defend the probusiness gains we made in congress in 2010 and that goal was achieved</s>
<s> the statement didnt explain the achievement but is likely referring to how republicans retained control of the house</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>we had also hoped to advance the probusiness position in the senate but the results were disappointing he added</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the chamber is one of the largest interest groups spending money on federal races and a longtime washington powerhouse</s>
<s>each year the chamber spends millions lobbying congress</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>over the past eight years the chamber has played a bigger role pushing to elect more businessfriendly candidates who tend to be republican  just as labor unions push to elect more workerfriendly candidates who tend to be democrats</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in 2010 the first election after a supreme court decision made it easier for companies to spend unlimited amounts of money the chamber spent $328 million</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>theyve become major players said stuart rothenberg editor and publisher of the rothenberg political report</s>
<s>they were very successful when republicans were successful last year and less successful this year when democrats were successful</s>
</p>

<p>
