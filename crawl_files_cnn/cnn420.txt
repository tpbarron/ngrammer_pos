<p>
<s>money magazine  how can you beat the odds if you want to join the boomerpreneur boom and start your own company after 50</s>
<s>money put that question to smallbusiness experts and dozens of fiftysomething entrepreneurs for their best advice</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this is the third of three articles on how to become a boomerpreneur</s>
<s>this one will help you understand the real costs of starting up a business and how to finance with caution</s>
<s>you can also see if youve got what it takes to own your own business and how to put time on your side and to see what lifestyle changes starting your own business will call for</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>after a deal to become a partner in the pharmacy where she worked fell through in 2009 sandi bryant then 45 decided to open a longterm care pharmacy service in fairview nc she projected startup costs of $300000</s>
<s>among other things that would require using $100000 from her 401k and putting her home up as collateral to secure $150000 in financing</s>
<s>understandably she and her husband were anxious there was a lot at stake if the business didnt take off</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>to see whether the risk would be worth it she painstakingly calculated how many prescriptions shed have to fill to break even</s>
<s>once i ran the numbers i was confident id see a profit in the first year she says and she was right americare pharmacy broke even after eight months</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>shes now paying herself a $150000 salary and expects to clear her debt in two years</s>
<s>as for her retirement shes again funding an ira and considers the business a big part of her nest egg</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>almost half of potential entrepreneurs ages 44 to 70 expect to use their personal savings to launch their ventures a survey by baby boomer think tank civic ventures found</s>
<s>while older owners often have more resources diverting those savings into a business is dicey</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its the opposite of diversification says colorado springs financial planner mary alpers</s>
<s>instead of dialing back your risk as you normally should at this age youre concentrating in something with a 50 chance of failure</s>
<s>whether you can afford to do so depends on how much you have and how flexible you can be</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>figure out the tab to get going</s>
<s>most entrepreneurs grossly underestimate startup costs says pewaukee wis financial planner kevin reardon</s>
<s>with less than 20 years to retirement however the effects of spending more than youd intended will be keenly felt</s>
<s>get a realistic appraisal by talking to owners of similar businesses</s>
<s>remember those startup costs include living expenses until you can pay yourself a sufficient salary a spouses job can help</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>see what youll be giving up</s>
<s>to decide how much skin you can put in the game youve got to look at the opportunity costs says mary beth izard startup consultant and author of boomerpreneurs</s>
<s>the biggest impact will likely be on your retirement</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>first use t rowe prices retirement income calculator to estimate what you can expect to collect after you leave the workforce based on your current situation</s>
<s>then run the numbers as if youd invested  and lost  your startup costs</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>are you willing to live on that or work longer to recover</s>
<s>you may also have to suspend saving for retirement</s>
<s>halting $15000 in annual contributions for three years at 50 will leave you with $114000 less at retirement assuming 6 annualized returns and quitting at 67 says alpers</s>
<s>at a 4 withdrawal rate thats $4400 less in year one more each year as you adjust for inflation</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>get the rest of the clan on board</s>
<s>when bob flynn and ray schofield launched greenride an environmentally friendly shuttle service it was an adjustment not just for them but for their families as well</s>
<s>both stopped saving for their childrens education</s>
<s>as a result schofields son took loans for college</s>
<s>flynns son will attend a state school and live at home to save money</s>
<s>not the college experience wed hoped for him says flynn</s>
<s>the lesson</s>
<s>understand that your new venture doesnt just mean tradeoffs for you</s>
<s>you have to look at how the business will affect your whole family says alpers</s>
<s>discuss ways in which youre willing to be flexible whether thats working longer or sending the kids to a public college  and whats nonnegotiable</s>
<s>make it clear that the tightfisted times wont last forever</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unable to fund greenride solely from their own pockets flynn and schofield struggled to line up financing</s>
<s>we approached a lot of banks but they said if youre not making money were not giving you a loan recalls flynn</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>they eventually turned to a banker they had worked with at their old jobs and secured an sbabacked loan for $250000</s>
<s>but both had to put up their homes as collateral</s>
<s>we put our lives on the line to do this says flynn</s>
<s>there was little room for error</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>have a small business question</s>
<s>ask the help desk</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>borrowing for your business can be just as damaging as tapping your savings</s>
<s>and tying your personal assets to your debt ratchets up your risk</s>
<s>flynn and schofield are paying down that debt from greenrides revenue but were still really exposed if the business fails says flynn</s>
<s>if you must borrow look for a better way</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>apply as a business</s>
<s>as long as your operation is a limited liability or s corporation the bank cant come after your assets if you default unless youve put them up as collateral</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it can be difficult to get a bank or an sbabacked loan unless youre making money</s>
<s>but a slew of new websites  biz2credit boefly and lendio  aim to pair small businesses with banks and credit unions that are eager to lend even to startups</s>
<s>by going this route you may also find lower rates than a big bank would offer</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>vendor financing is another option ask the companies you do business with for a line of credit pharmacy owner sandi bryant got a $100000 one from a drug company</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>go to your network</s>
<s>if youre turned down for a business loan consider tapping friends and family</s>
<s>at this age youre more likely to have a network with deep pockets says lesa mitchell vice president at the kauffman foundation</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>lay out the terms on paper</s>
<s>while you have to pay the minimum interest rate set by the irs  search index of applicable federal rates at irsgov  thats lower than bank rates</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>reduce your exposure</s>
<s>rather than use your home as collateral ask a friend or relative if they would be willing to guarantee the loan as a cosigner</s>
<s>or pool resources with a partner so you can borrow less</s>
<s>if you must put your own assets on the line make paying back those loans a priority</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as for how much debt to take on think about your monthly payments the sba guideline is that debt payments should be no more than half your businesss net income</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>stephanie ringer who now runs workshop the creative workplace borrowed about half of the $64000 she needed to get the corporate meeting space started plus used a $25000 line of credit to cover some firstyear costs</s>
<s>the money coming in from existing clients enabled her to pay the loan back almost immediately</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>five years later workshop has grown steadily</s>
<s>ringer has a chief numbers guru to handle finances a director of lasting impressions for customer service and a director of possibilities on sales</s>
<s>and she is expanding into giving speeches and private coaching</s>
<s>i like being in charge of my destiny she says</s>
<s>i wont work for anyone else again</s>
</p>
