<s>stephanie ringer 51 louisville ky</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  as a regional director for aflac insurance stephanie ringer had built her louisville sales team into one of the top in kentucky</s>
<s>one of her secrets for keeping her staff motivated</s>
<s>holding brainstorming sessions in a local meeting space called workshop</s>
<s>she found that the center  with its whiteboards comfy couches and crazy toys like hula hoops  fueled productive sessions</s>
<s>so when workshops owners put the business up for sale in 2007 ringer then 46 decided to buy it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>family and friends were surprised at her willingness to take such a risky step at that point in her career</s>
<s>everyone said are you sure this is a good idea</s>
<s>  she recalls</s>
<s>but i felt in my gut it was right</s>
<s> with decades of leadership experience behind her she was sure she could run a successful business</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>five years later ringer has six employees and nearly $300000 in annual revenue</s>
<s>while she drew only a $30000 salary last year she expects to earn $60000 this year and clear six figures by 2013</s>
<s>most important she loves being the boss  or as she calls herself chief fun officer</s>
<s>this is the best career move ive ever made she says</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>when you think of todays entrepreneurs young hotshots like facebooks mark zuckerberg or googles larry page may be the faces that come to mind</s>
<s>but its baby boomers like ringer who are striking out on their own at the fastest clip of any generation</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>americans 55 to 64 have launched more businesses than any other age group during the past decade closely followed by those 45 to 54 reports the kauffman foundation a nonprofit dedicated to entrepreneurship</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for some the change has been forced upon them by the tough job market</s>
<s>others are taking the leap to escape boredom become their own boss pursue a passion  or simply in hopes of hitting it rich</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>whatever the motivation entrepreneurs face a tough road</s>
<s>half of businesses fail within the first five years the us small business administration reports</s>
<s>some research shows that boomerpreneurs have more staying power than younger folks but the stakes are also higher you have little time to recover from failure you have higher living expenses to cover you may have a family depending on your income and though youve likely amassed more wealth youre closer to the time when you need to tap those assets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so how can you beat the odds if you want to join the boomerpreneur boom</s>
<s>money put that question to smallbusiness experts and dozens of fiftysomething entrepreneurs for their best advice</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this is the first of three articles on how to become a boomerpreneur</s>
<s>this one will help you to see if youve got what it takes to own your own business and how to put time on your side</s>
<s>you can also get tips for changing your lifestyle knowing the real costs of starting up a business and financing with caution</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>after discovering shops in europe dedicated to gourmet olive oils and vinegars jim milligan dreamed of opening a similar store with tasting rooms</s>
<s>then living outside the twin cities he thought such a business would do well in a vacation destination like traverse city mich where hed often spent time with his inlaws</s>
<s>getting laid off at age 55 in 2007 from his position as a general manager at 3m gave him the push he needed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>those first few years running fustinis  named for the italian word for the stainlesssteel containers used to store olive oil  were harder than hed expected</s>
<s>milligan put in 80hour weeks</s>
<s>i was on my feet a lot he says</s>
<s>between hiring staff managing inventory and working the register he was more mentally and physically taxed than ever before</s>
<s>but hes quick to add its been incredibly satisfying too</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>no matter what field you go into youll probably find that owning a business requires more multitasking risk taking and stress management than your old job did</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the beginning at least youll be ceo secretary and everything in between  expect long days on the job and sleepless nights ruminating over whatifs</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>you have to consider your health your stamina your tolerance for risk and whether this is something you really want to do at this stage of life says colorado springs financial planner mary alpers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>do a selfassessment</s>
<s>successful entrepreneurs are calculatedrisk takers says mary beth izard startup consultant and author of boomerpreneurs</s>
<s>look back on your career to see how well that describes you have you set high goals for yourself</s>
<s>are you fiercely competitive always looking to whats next</s>
<s>have you thrived amid uncertainty</s>
<s>do you enjoy making decisions</s>
<s>are you quick to adapt</s>
<s>most important have you maintained your drive as youve aged</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if you cant say yes to most of those questions the entrepreneurial life isnt for you</s>
<s>to be fair not all businesses require the same amount of chutzpah</s>
<s>a freelance graphic designer wont face the same pressures that a restaurateur with high overhead and a big staff does</s>
<s>you can match a business with your risk tolerance suggests izard</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>make sure youre in love</s>
<s>unless youre 100 enthusiastic about your product or service youll resent the time you put in</s>
<s>passion on the other hand can sustain you through long hours and energize you in ways you havent experienced in years says lesa mitchell vice president at the kauffman foundation</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>should you be forced into entrepreneurship look for a niche that motivates you</s>
<s>if youre leaving a job find ways to put your fervor to the test before quitting</s>
<s>before opening a café for example shadow a restaurateur to see whether youd like it as much as you think</s>
<s>try out your idea in your spare time before launching it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>put time on your side</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>marguerite cole had long wanted to start her own business but figured shed wait until age 60 when her home would be paid off</s>
<s>when a 2009 restructuring at microsoft cost her her job as director of sales strategy however the then47yearold moved up her plan</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with only six months of severance and a monthly mortgage bill she needed income fast</s>
<s>since she had lots of contacts she decided to launch a businessstrategy consulting firm</s>
<s>thanks to low overhead ive been able to pay myself a salary every month since starting says the redmond wash resident</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>have a question about starting a business</s>
<s>ask the help desk</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as cole was acutely aware older entrepreneurs dont have the many years a twentysomething has to succeed let alone recover from failure</s>
<s>so put in legwork to make sure your venture will be among the 50 that survive  and to ensure that youll be thriving soon</s>
<s>the average business takes three years to show a profit the sba reports</s>
<s>shoot for under that average so that you dont have to lean too heavily on your savings says planner alpers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>aim for a quick profit</s>
<s>buying an existing company as stephanie ringer did will get you up and running faster than starting from scratch</s>
<s>or aim for a business thats not capitalintensive à la cole and the other 5 million boomers who are consultants according to smallbusiness services provider mbo partners</s>
<s>most start up with less than $5000</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>have a more ambitious idea in mind</s>
<s>run a breakeven analysis  find help at sbagov  to see whether youll profit within three years</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>do what you know  or learn before you launch</s>
<s>if youre starting a business within your area of expertise the likelihood of success will be higher says izard</s>
<s>youll be able to leverage your network and years of experience</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>going boldly into new territory is especially risky in your fifties</s>
<s>but if youre set on it familiarize yourself with the field first get training attend conferences find a mentor</s>
<s>or bring in a collaborator with the knowledge you lack</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>have a plan b</s>
<s>even careful planning wont guarantee results</s>
<s>set a limit now on how long youll give the venture to meet your projections based on your resources</s>
<s>also think about what youll do if the business doesnt survive</s>
<s>go back to your old employer</s>
<s>retire early</s>
<s>stay connected with former bosses and colleagues in case you need them</s>
</p>
