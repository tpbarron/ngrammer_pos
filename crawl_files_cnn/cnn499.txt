<p>
<s>new york money magazine  this story is part of money magazines retirement guide 2013 where youll find strategies to guide you through the last stretch starting at 10 years out then five years one year and finally your first year of leisure</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>welcome to your first year of retirement</s>
<s>you made it</s>
<s>hard work and diligent saving have paid off</s>
<s>your financial plan should practically run itself at this point</s>
<s>still aim to check in periodically</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>what to do</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>dont go chasing yield</s>
<s>as you age the fixedincome portion of your portfolio becomes more important</s>
<s>the goal isnt maximum income but maximum preservation  by way of diversification</s>
<s>so put the majority of these holdings into a total bond market index fund</s>
<s>for inflation protection add tips keeping them to less than 30 of your bond share</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>anyone who has substantial money outside taxdeferred accounts and is in a high tax bracket should consider munis too</s>
<s>initially youll also keep 12 months of expenses plus your emergency fund in cash</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>every couple of years trim a few percentage points from your stockholdings and stick the money in bonds and cash</s>
<s>by 75 you should have two to three years expenses in a moneymarket or shortterm bond fund</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at that point you dont need as much growth to keep up with inflation says greg carroll managing partner at sterling wealth management in carlsbad calif</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>do a yearly spending checkup</s>
<s>before you quit working you gave yourself a budget</s>
<s>expect to blow it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>retiring is like going on a long vacation and you always spend more on vacation says wealth adviser jeff townsend</s>
<s>besides your costs of living will naturally vary as will your portfolios value</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related whats the minimum amount youd need to retire today</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>townsend suggests tracking your spending once annually to keep yourself honest</s>
<s>go back to the budget worksheet on fidelitys retirement income planner</s>
<s>then plug in your assets to see if your spending is sustainable</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>chronically going over a 4 inflationadjusted draw could cause your money to run out but even if youve gone off the rails in a few years dialing back can make a difference</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>take from all baskets</s>
<s>as you spend down your cash account youll need to replenish it</s>
<s>minimizing the taxes you incur on portfolio withdrawals will maximize the life span of your savings</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>generally retirees have been advised to tap taxable assets first because the longterm capital gains rate on them is lower than the income tax rate owed on traditional 401k and ira withdrawals and because this method allows taxdeferred accounts to continue to grow without a tax bite</s>
<s>whether or not the strategy works however depends on many variables</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>another failing once you do transition to drawing solely on taxdeferred accounts you may be bumped into a higher tax bracket says colorado springs financial planner and cpa allan roth</s>
<s>not to mention that all this becomes even more complicated with possible taxrate changes for 2013 says roth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related 25 best places to retire</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while theres no one perfect system roth suggests being more egalitarian with your drawdown</s>
<s>start by balancing any tax deductions you have with withdrawals from taxdeferred accounts then take the rest of the money you need from taxable accounts</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>never retire your resume</s>
<s>keep in mind that a worstcase scenario may necessitate your returning to work</s>
<s>submitting a cv that hasnt been dusted off since y2k wont do you any favors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>so update your résumé now while recent accomplishments are fresh in your mind says new york city executive recruiter steve viscusi</s>
<s>then revisit it once a year to add something even if its volunteer work or leadership in a social club</s>
</p>
