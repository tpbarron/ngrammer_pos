<p>
<s>retirement planning is a journey too so the same concept applies</s>
<s>once you have a retirement goal in mind you want to be able to refer to benchmarks along the way to see how youre doing</s>
<s>otherwise you could find yourself at the end of your career well short of the savings youll need</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the best yardstick is the size of your nest egg relative to your income</s>
<s>to quit working at 65 with a decent shot at replacing 80 of your preretirement earnings youll need savings equal to roughly 12 times your income that assumes youll collect social security but no pension</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>along the way says financial planner charles farrell author of your money ratios 8 simple tools for financial security you should aim to hit these milestones by 35 you should have 14 times your pay tucked away</s>
<s>that ratio is 37 at 45 and 71 at 55</s>
<s>calculator are you saving enough</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>farrells math assumes you save 12 to 15 of your salary a year including matches and that your investments beat inflation by 45 percentage points a year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>i imagine some of you are now saying whoa</s>
<s>am i doomed to a grim retirement</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>no</s>
<s>falling short of these figures isnt necessarily cause for alarm</s>
<s>ratcheting up your savings rate may be enough to make up for lost ground</s>
<s>granted that requires a concerted effort</s>
<s>but once youve caught up you can ease back to a savings rate of 15 of pay</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>even if you cant catch up you neednt despair</s>
<s>there isnt a single path to one retirement destination</s>
<s>by living on 70 of your salary or working a few more years you can cut the savings levels you must reach by 10 to 25</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>of course life doesnt necessarily work out so neatly that you can consistently hit any series of savings targets</s>
<s>more likely your income and expenses will have ups and downs throughout your career so that you fall behind in lean times but have the opportunity to catch up when youre flush</s>
</p>

<p>
