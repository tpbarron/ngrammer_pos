<p>
<s>find a handyman to take on those home improvement projects you just cant seem to get to</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  for a busy  or simply hammerphobic  homeowner it sounds almost too good to be true a hired handyman or woman could tackle your odd jobs for about a third of the cost time and hassle of a general contractor</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>there are plenty of jacksofalltrades out there but is it possible to find one you can really rely on</s>
<s>the answer is yes  provided you know where to look and what chores to give him</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>pick the right project</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a handyman should do only things that a proficient homeowner could handle himself if he had the time says handyman fran carito of watertown mass</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that might include installing a ceiling fan freeing a stuck window sash or replacing a light fixture faucet or cracked windowpane</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>go with a licensed tradesman instead for work inside walls like replacing a pipe or adding an electrical circuit specialized tasks say repairing a roof leak or installing tiles and of course remodeling</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>tap your network</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>general contractors dont usually like to bother with small fixup tasks anyway so if you have a good working rapport with one he might be willing to suggest one of his guys who takes side jobs</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>or ask a contact at a local school church or office park to recommend a staff handyman who does some moonlighting</s>
<s>otherwise check with friends for referrals</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related 10 ways to get yard sale deals</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a longestablished local sole proprietor may be the most expert and reasonably priced option</s>
<s>a larger company though is more likely to carry insurance especially if its part of a national franchise such as mr handyman or house doctors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>get an estimate</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unlike contractors who name their price upfront handymen bill you for the materials they use and the time they spend  typically at around $40 to $70 an hour</s>
<s>thats because ironically its trickier to commit to a fixed price for a little job says madison handyman adam shirley</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related what your home is really worth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>still ask for an estimate  and consider setting a ceiling</s>
<s>for instance you could designate a halfhour for trying to repair a dripping faucet before replacing it instead</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>start small</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>just as you might audition a car mechanic with routine oil changes test your new handymans mettle with lowtech stuff such as clearing clogged gutters or installing garage shelves</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its best to combine a few of these small projects since most pros either have a two to fourhour minimum or impose a onehour service charge</s>
<s>then let him earn his way to bigger assignments that require craftsmanship  and if he nails those put his number on speed dial</s>
</p>
