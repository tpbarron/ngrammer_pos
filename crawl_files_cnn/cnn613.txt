<p>
<s>tune your strategy dont let the european debt crisis or chinas slowdown strike a discordant note in your portfolio</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>money magazine  incomestarved investors looking to amp up their portfolios have been turning to foreign bonds  for obvious reasons</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the 42 payout on the average foreign fixedincome fund dwarfs the yield on us bonds by about 1½ percentage points</s>
<s>lately though that extra income has come at a steep price</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as interest rates and currencies have swung violently amid the global slowdown and european credit crisis  yields on 10year italian debt have gone from 73 to 48 and back up to 58 in the past nine months  foreign bond funds have been rocked</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>spdr barclays capital international treasury bond etf bwx which owns mostly foreign government debt is down 4 since last september more than wiping out its yield from the prior year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>emergingmarket bond funds have been even more skittish losing 46 just in may on worries that chinas slowdown will crimp growth in the developing world</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related chinese manufacturing slumps to 9month low</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>given that bonds are supposed to add ballast  not instability  to your portfolio it raises the question whether foreign debt is worth the trouble</s>
<s>yes  as long as you adhere to certain guidelines</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>cap your foreign exposure</s>
<s>tony rodriguez cohead of fixed income at nuveen investments says investors ultimately need the global diversification and yields that foreign securities offer</s>
<s>but he recommends limiting this stake to around 15 of your bond portfolio</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>since 1985 this conservative allocation has captured roughly 96 of the returns of a more aggressive 40 foreign60 domestic bond strategy while being 20 less rocky according to morningstar</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hedge your currency bets</s>
<s>its not just the added yield thats given foreign bonds a boost</s>
<s>so too has the longterm decline of the us dollar which pads the gains of americans investing abroad</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>between 1972 and 2011 a basket of bonds from 19 foreign markets returned an annualized 46 according to a study by credit suisse</s>
<s>however if those investments had been hedged  a process where fund managers sell the same amount of foreign currencies they purchase to buy foreign bonds  that same basket would have gained 31 annually</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>whats the point of hedging then</s>
<s>because doing so produced a portfolio that bounced up and down 60 less than an unhedged approach over the past 40 years</s>
<s>plus in case you havent noticed the dollar has been strengthening over the past year largely because global investors view dollardenominated assets as safe havens</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>dont think this trend will hold</s>
<s>or maybe mitigating risk isnt your sole goal</s>
<s>then stick with unhedged developedmarket funds</s>
<s>but consider hedging funds that own emergingmarket debt since theres greater volatility in those markets and currencies says kathy jones fixedincome strategist at the schwab center for financial research</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>you can do so through a fund like fidelity new markets income fnmix</s>
<s>john carlson morningstars bond manager of the year in 2011 limits this funds foreign currency exposure to no more than 20</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>think active management</s>
<s>most bond indexes base their country exposure on the total value of global debt</s>
<s>that means index funds will expose you mostly to the biggest countries and debtors</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the top two allocations for the spcitigroup international treasury bond index for instance are japan and germany whose 10year bonds are yielding 08 and 13  hardly catnip for income investors</s>
<s>also in its top five are bonds from debtplagued italy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related eurozone nearing another recession</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the longterm fundamentals in smaller economies are where we see value says peter wilson comanager of the wells fargo advantage international bond fund</s>
<s>among the biggest country allocations for his $17 billion portfolio are canada and australia</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>canada has less debt as a percentage of gross domestic product than the us while australias economy is on pace to grow 34 this year vs just 2 for the us plus australias 10year bonds are yielding about 1½ points more than treasuries  sweet music for income seekers</s>
</p>
