<p>
<s>most affluent parents say little to their older kids about money</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york money magazine  not long after my father passed away in 1999 my mother insisted on giving me a thorough accounting of her finances</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>on a routine visit without warning she handed me a plain manila envelope containing a list of all her assets and account numbers and values contact information for her lawyer and what felt like heartless instructions on accessing and emptying the safedeposit box</s>
<s>among them go to the bank before contacting the medical examiner</s>
<s> uh thanks mom</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>even at the tender age of 21 i realized this level of disclosure was unusual</s>
<s>but the contrast really kicked in years later as i learned from friends and my spouse how seldom money was discussed in their homes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>weve had conversations about having conversations about money but they stalled before getting anywhere says my husband kimon</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>thats definitely more the norm according to recent studies</s>
<s>only 32 of the boomers in a new survey from us trust for instance had fully disclosed their financial situation to their kids and just 44 of parents over 67 had</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if youre among this tightlipped majority you no doubt have your reasons</s>
<s>maybe as the research has commonly found you were raised not to discuss money or if youre doing well financially you worry that full disclosure could have a negative effect on your childrens work ethic</s>
<s>or perhaps you simply havent gotten around to having the talk</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>understand this though keeping certain key details about your money to yourself can be costly both emotionally and financially  an outcome you may be familiar with from dealing with your own parents</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your adult children could be worrying about you unnecessarily or they may be unprepared to help with your affairs if the need suddenly arises</s>
<s>and they may have unrealistic expectations about how much or little youre willing and able to help them with their finances</s>
<s>generally the more open you can be the better says baltimore financial planner tim maurer</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that doesnt mean you have to get into nittygritty numbers that you may prefer to keep private</s>
<s>my mother may be comfortable sharing her bank balance and portfolio value but you dont have to as long as youre open about certain essentials</s>
<s>the following guidelines can help you identify whats truly critical for your kids to know  and what isnt necessarily any of their business</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the least they need to know</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>to ensure your children are able to honor your wishes if you become incapacitated and when you die there are some details you must discuss says dan brady an estate attorney in raleigh nc  especially if one or more of your offspring are likely to be the ones responsible for executing your plans</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this as it turns out was a motivating factor for my moms tellall</s>
<s>her sister mother and husband had passed in quick succession making her more aware of her own mortality she explained recently</s>
<s>i wanted you to know if anything happened to me that this information would be your bible she said adding that my being an only child necessitated promoting me to partner in her arrangements</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related baby boomers dont count on a fat inheritance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as my mother did you can start cluing your kids in as soon as they reach adulthood says brady</s>
<s>theres no need to go into the same level of detail with each one you know best who can handle what but they should all know the same basics to avoid future rifts</s>
<s>the three mustshares</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your estate plans</s>
<s>let your children know whether you have wills health and financial directives and funeral arrangements plus where to find the relevant documents</s>
<s>also provide contact info for your attorney financial planner broker and accountant</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>clients say youll tell my kids everything they need to know when i die right</s>
<s>  says colleen barney an irvine calif estate lawyer</s>
<s>sure but they need to know to notify me first that the parent has died</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your childs role</s>
<s>when naming your executor or giving someone power of attorney its best to ask your top candidate if he or she is up for the task says brady</s>
<s>already had your kids name inked in legalese</s>
<s>make sure junior is aware of the role and what it requires giving him a tacit opportunity to back out if necessary</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the toughest conversation especially when you give an adult child your health care proxy you have to talk about how you feel about life support says brady whos seen cases in which the authorized offspring couldnt follow through with the parents wishes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>where to find your assets</s>
<s>make a list of your bank and investment accounts real estate holdings and insurance policies particularly life and longterm care</s>
<s>dont forget the safedeposit box whats in it where its at who has access</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>not comfortable giving all this info to the children</s>
<s>store it with your financial adviser or attorney then let the kids know the list is there</s>
<s>if you become incapacitated your children will need access to this info to help you notes barney</s>
<s>i have clients in their nineties who cant take care of themselves yet their kids have no idea how the finances are set up she says</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the list will also make it easier for your children to settle your affairs after your death by expediting the dispensation of your assets and ensuring that stray accounts dont go missing and ultimately end up in state coffers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>what else to tell them about you</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>think about what you needed to know about your parents money</s>
<s>probably topping the list was whether they had resources to live comfortably through retirement and pay for care if they got sick</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>guess what</s>
<s>your kids want to know the same about you  especially if theyve seen you fretting over grandpas finances</s>
<s>take note one in two adult children of baby boomers is worried that his parents wont be secure in retirement a recent ameriprise study found</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>now that im older and my mom is in her early seventies a fact shell surely be thrilled to see in print im comforted to know all i do about her finances as it tells me that she has funds to provide for her modest lifestyle</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>monument colo financial planner mary alpers says she wishes more of her clients would communicate their sense of security to their children</s>
<s>its one of the best gifts they can give</s>
<s> how to have that conversation depends on where youre at</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if you think youll be able to remain independent you dont need to tell your children your 401k balance the size of your pension or the value of your other assets</s>
<s>you do however want to offer some evidence beyond a vague dont worry about us</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>to do that let them know where your money will come from in retirement  for example you might say you have a goodsize 401k plus emergency savings to supplement social security suggests columbus financial planner jill gianola</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>add that youve run calculations to make sure youre on track and have found that you should be able to cover your daytoday expenses indefinitely after you stop working with some left over for unexpected bills and fun</s>
<s>fill them in on whether youve bought longtermcare insurance to cover nursinghome costs in case you become ill or how you plan to pay otherwise</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if you work with a financial adviser and feel comfortable doing so you could also let your child look over the projections that person has run showing your ability to support yourselves in retirement</s>
<s>or you might arrange a family meeting with your planner to convey the message</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if youre not sure youll be okay or dont know uneasy at the notion of talking with your kids because you fear your savings are inadequate</s>
<s>maybe you feel embarrassed and dont want them to worry about how youll manage or whether theyll need to support you one day</s>
<s>take your discomfort as a wakeup call urges alpers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>first run the numbers to see how big a shortfall you could be facing</s>
<s>do the results confirm the worst</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>if youre still employed quickly kick up your retirementaccount contributions to powersave your way into a better situation</s>
<s>as long as youre aggressively working to improve your prospects and you have time to truly alter the outcome you can hold off filling in the kids for a while</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>those already in retirement probably need to focus more on cutting expenses  perhaps downsizing to a smaller home or even moving to a lowercost area</s>
<s>and as tough as the conversation may be you should let your children know your situation</s>
<s>they may have suggestions and if they want and are able to offer financial help when needed they can plan accordingly</s>
<s>in this case says gianola sharing the numbers becomes more important</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>what to tell them about them</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>parents who intend to leave money to their kids or help with a big expense may be reluctant to talk about their plans partly out of concern theyll compromise the childs work ethic the us trust survey revealed</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>thats one refrain i heard from my inlaws when i asked why theyd been reticent about money with their sons they didnt want to build dependencies</s>
<s>id like our children to make it in the same way we did my motherinlaw elaine explained</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>parents may be right to worry</s>
<s>when someone knows theyll get money that is not attached to work they do that can kill their drive to succeed says psychologist and financial planner brad klontz coauthor of mind over money</s>
<s> this is most true he adds for young adults in their twenties and thirties who arent settled in their careers and havent yet developed good savings habits</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related will an inheritance hurt our chances for financial aid</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>my fatherinlaw vassilis also worried about judgments the boys may make or expectations they may develop</s>
<s>a good point given that you might have a few million today but what if the market tanks tomorrow</s>
<s>or you need extended nursing care</s>
<s>or decide you really want a ferrari spyder</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>experts say these concerns are reasonable but some advance notice can also help your children prepare for the future</s>
<s>staking out the right middle ground depends on the gift you have in mind</s>
<s>heres what to say about </s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your plans to help while youre still around</s>
<s>intending to say pay for a chunk of your grandkids tuition</s>
<s>keep it to yourself until youre certain about what assistance you can give says rapid city sd financial planner rick kahler coauthor of wired for wealth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>otherwise children may anchor on a vague promise</s>
<s>you say honey my intention is to help you pay for graduate school but they may hear im going to pay for graduate school  he says</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>not only do you risk disappointing junior but you could also undercut his motivation to save for the part you didnt intend to pay for</s>
<s>in this case youd be better off specifying the number of years youll cover or the amount of money youre planning to give</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>your plans to leave them money</s>
<s>unless youre so rich that theres no scenario in which you could run out of money dont promise your kid an inheritance in your fifties or sixties</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for most boomers there are too many unknowns particularly about your health says alpers</s>
<s>once you get to your midseventies your children are older your financial situation is more known and your chance of living another 30 years is slim so you can be more open</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>letting your child know then that you hope to leave her money provides an opening to talk about how youd like the funds used</s>
<s>just dont be definitive about the dollars</s>
<s>say something like we think there might be money left over for you when we die</s>
<s>we cant give you a specific amount because our circumstances could change but we want you to know its a possibility</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>while youre at it clarify anything that could come as a surprise in your will such as disproportionate bequests</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>suspicion over why mom left your sister 80 and you 20 is the kind of thing that breaks up families says estate attorney dan brady</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related kids average $15 a week in allowance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>frame the conversation in terms of your values says barbara nusbaum a new york psychologist</s>
<s>for example im so glad i was able to give you the down payment for your house</s>
<s>to be fair since i didnt do the same for your sister im planning to leave her more in my will</s>
<s>i hope you understand</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in fact the values you convey in any of these conversations about money may be your childs most important takeaway</s>
</p>

<s>looking back i realize that envelope from my mom contained a lot more than financial data  the contents demonstrated to me the rewards of saving diligently as my parents did while the act of passing the information showed me how much my mom prizes financial security and independence</s>

<s>thats a pretty rich gift in itself  one im grateful to have now since with any luck itll be decades before i have to open that safedeposit box</s>
