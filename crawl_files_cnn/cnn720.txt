<p>
<s>the fledgling companies including taskrabbit agent anything and zaarly let you turn your neighbors into personal assistants spontaneously tapping people in your community to perform jobs that are as selfindulgent and arbitrary as you wish</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>taskmasters have paid gofers $200 to wait in a long line for a new ipad and $60 to set up ikea furniture</s>
<s>one man in austin shelled out $100 for a latenight cheeseburger delivery</s>
<s>another offered $10 to have someone write a thankyou note to his parents</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its regular people calling on regular people explains bo fishback the founder and ceo of zaarlycom one of the newest of these taskoriented startups</s>
<s>the 10employee san francisco company launched in march and landed $1 million from investors including actor ashton kutcher former google goog fortune 500 exec paul buchheit and silicon valley venture firm felicis ventures</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>to use zaarly clients provide their locations propose tasks and deadlines and state how much theyre willing to pay</s>
<s>zaarly broadcasts each message on its website on twitter and on its smartphone app</s>
<s>tasks appear on a map labeled with dollar signs inviting applicants to respond and say why theyre right for the job</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>zaarly posts go beyond errands</s>
<s>theyve included requests to buy a used saxophone or unused broadway theater tickets or to pay for an experience like riding in a car that goes from zero to 60 miles per hour in four seconds</s>
<s>that person offered $50 to go for a spin</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>zaarly connects individual buyers and sellers over the phone through an anonymous party line</s>
<s>when a match is made the two people can arrange to meet facetoface to complete the transaction</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>zaarly takes a 99 transaction fee for processing electronic payments through its system but users who opt to exchange cash instead avoid the surcharge</s>
<s>about 40000 people are now using zaarly in boston chicago los angeles new york san francisco seattle and washington dc fishback says the service should be available nationwide by the years end</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>zaarly is one of a gaggle of ondemand errand services that have appeared in the last six months capitalizing on the rise of mobile apps gps technology and social networking</s>
<s>each has its own twist</s>
<s>they range from agent anything which enlists 1800 students at columbia university and new york university to do missions for new yorkers to chicagobased airrun a new smartphone app for connecting seekers and fetchers nationwide</s>
<s>virtual concierge site fancyhandscom offers monthly $35 to $55 subscriptions that allow you to outsource a monthly quota of 15 tasks that can be done remotely such as scheduling a friday haircut or putting together a spreadsheet</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>israeli startup fiverrcom lets users post what theyll do for $5 whether its making a 30second video or getting yucko the clown to prank call and yell at your friends</s>
<s>another startup in san francisco called heyneighborcom launched in early july to connect neighbors for free or paid favors like watering your plants or moving a couch</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>critics worry about the safety of such peertopeer networks pointing to the spate of crimes perpetrated using craigslist</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>startup executives however see the biggest challenge in just getting enough users on both sides of the equation  the pool of both bidders and gofers  to make their services worthwhile</s>
<s>taskrabbit and heyneighbor are tackling the problem by focusing on key major markets while airrun is taking a go at building a national brand immediately</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>none of the startups contacted for this article would disclose their revenues</s>
<s>all believe their business models will prove profitable</s>
<s>to get there theyll need to spark widespread adoption of their services to the point where each task can reach a small army of willing and wellsituated gofers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>zaarly and taskrabbit intend to make money immediately their peertopeer economy with transaction fees</s>
<s>that revenue model will be key to survival says jake wengroff social media research director at frost  sullivan</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>theres going to be a shakeout he says</s>
<s>theres probably room for at most three providers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it will be especially tough if after their initial meetings buyers and sellers decide to then work together privately offline he says</s>
<s>that revenue will disappear wengroff says</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>none of the gofers are making a living off performing such services yet according to zaarlys fishback  though he knows two college students who decided to bid for zaarly tasks instead of getting traditional summer jobs</s>
