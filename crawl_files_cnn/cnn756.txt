<s>rokus $99 streaming stick plugs into tv sets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fortune  anthony wood is not your typical silicon valley pitch man</s>
<s>the mildmannered ceo of streamingmedia startup roku has a penchant for slow pauseladen speech</s>
<s>he tends to pluck his corded suspenders when hes finally made his point</s>
<s>but this gentle demeanor hasnt stopped wood 46 from besting much larger rivals including google goog fortune 500 and sony sne</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>roku based in saratoga calif makes puckshaped devices that beam media  television shows movies and sports  from the internet onto tvs</s>
<s>since the first roku player made its debut in 2008 the company has built up 650 channels from netflix nflx to nba game time and sold over 3 million players</s>
<s>combined roku and apples aapl fortune 500similar box apple tv command about 95 of the market for internet tv streaming devices the company claims</s>
<s>earlier this year roku raised a $45 million round of funding from backers including menlo ventures and news corp nwsa fortune 500</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>wood has an impressive track record</s>
<s>in the mid1990s he wanted to find a better way to record reruns of his favorite tv show star trek the next generation</s>
<s>vhs tapes werent cutting it</s>
<s>so he invented a programmable settop box with a small hard drive that gave show listings and could even pause live tv</s>
<s>it was the first digital video recorder or dvr</s>
<s>he founded replaytv a company eventually bought by directv dtv fortune 500</s>
<s>dvrs have become nearly ubiquitous and are poised to reach some 338 million households globally by 2018</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in 2007 wood was recruited by netflix ceo reed hastings to build the netflix player a box that beamed content directly to tvs</s>
<s>hastings eventually nixed the idea in order to coax other companies into making hardware with builtin netflix support</s>
<s>wood with a $6 million investment from hastings released the design on his own as the first roku streamingvideo device</s>
<s>roku japanese for six is woods sixth company</s>
<s>anthony isnt a virgin to the business hastings tells fortune</s>
<s>hes succeeded at multiple different companies</s>
<s>thats very rare</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>more tv not required  5 shows to watch online</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>now wood is contemplating getting rid of the box altogether</s>
<s>rokus newest product the streaming stick looks like a usb memory drive and plugs directly into the back of newer tv sets turning them into smart televisions</s>
<s>the gadget streams content like a typical roku player and allows features to be added in the future</s>
<s>wood calculates that tv makers will bundle the device to sell more sets without significantly driving up their costs</s>
<s>the $99 streaming stick went on sale in october</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>nobody knows what will happen with online television</s>
<s>content providers and technology firms are placing wildly different bets</s>
<s>and examples of stinging failures abound</s>
<s>googles recently released $299 digitalmediastreaming device the orbshaped nexus q was widely panned</s>
<s>they blew it says wood</s>
<s> and oncehot startups such as boxee have had to change course</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>roku has avoided such pitfalls in part by aggressively undercutting competitors</s>
<s>its devices start at just $49</s>
<s> but the company is not yet profitable and has spent a lot to advertise</s>
<s>presented with those challenges wood shrugs noting that roku has already managed to adapt quickly</s>
<s>whatever happens he vows well be ready</s>
</p>
