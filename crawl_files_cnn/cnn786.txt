<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  since its 2010 debut no tablet has come close to unseating the mighty ipad</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the many contenders can all be tossed in two piles me too devices or cheapersmaller tablets</s>
<s>neither has dealt apple aapl fortune 500 a serious blow and the ipad mini  unveiled on tuesday  is likely to solidify apples dominance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but windows 8 offers a compelling third way to take on the ipad by putting a full pc experience on a tablet</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>most tablets currently on the market are complementary devices</s>
<s>they have barebones operating systems that make onthego media consumption and web browsing a cinch</s>
<s>for most users they havent yet replaced the need for a pc</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>few people create documents spreadsheets or presentations on their ipads and even fewer run any serious business applications on them</s>
<s>todays tablets dont multitask well and it departments that want to dig deep into the operating system to customize settings arent going to have much luck</s>
<s>you still need a pc to do that</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the solution windows 8 offers is inspired  and controversial</s>
<s>the new operating system which goes on sale friday has two modes the start screen filled with large app tiles fullscreen apps and hidden menu functions and the more traditional desktop mode with smaller app icons taskbars and menu ribbons</s>
<s>both modes work with touch or a mouse</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>unlike apple which makes separate operating systems for mobile devices and macs microsoft msft fortune 500 thinks users want a full no compromises pc experience on their tablets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>will buyers follow microsofts lead</s>
<s>analysts are divided and the range of estimates is staggering</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ihs isuppli forecasts that there will be nearly 20 million tablets in use by the end of next year that run either windows 8 or windows rt a stripped down version of windows 8 that does not support legacy windows applications</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that would be a nice start but its nowhere near the 73 million ipads gartner expects apple to sell next year</s>
<s>there will be 118 million ipad users by then ihs estimates</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>other analysts are far more conservative</s>
<s>forrester research thinks windows 8 and rt will be on a combined 7 million tablets by the end of next year</s>
<s>idc is even more pessimistic forecasting that just under 5 million windows tablets will be in use by the end of 2013</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>though its immediate prospects seem bleak most analysts think windows will eventually gain steam</s>
<s>by 2016 ihs isuppli thinks windows will control 25 of the tablet market  a strong competitor to apple which will still control nearly half the market down from its 70 share today</s>
<s>forrester predicts that microsoft will have a 27 share of the tablet market in 2016 compared to 53 for apple</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>windows tablets will need beautiful hardware a robust app store and aggressive marketing to really take on the ipad</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>microsoft needs to make users want to see the products pick them up and touch their screens says michael silver analyst at gartner</s>

<p>

<s>developers wont build windows apps until millions of customers have mobile windows devices and customers are leery of buying devices that lack popular apps</s>

<p>
