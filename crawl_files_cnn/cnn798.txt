<p>
<s>erskine bowles and alan simpson cochairs of president obamas debt commission launched a new campaign called fix the debt one goal get 10 million signatures calling on congress to act</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  a new campaign launched tuesday aims to create public support for lawmakers who have been too afraid to back a bipartisan debtreduction plan</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a key goal of the fix the debt campaign is to get 10 million signatures for an online petition that will call on congress to pass a comprehensive longterm plan by july 4 2013</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>fix the debt was created by erskine bowles and alan simpson who cochaired president obamas debt reduction commission in 2010</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>besides doing outreach to educate the public the campaign will encourage lawmakers to use the bowlessimpson plan as a starting point for their negotiations this year and next</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that plan would reduce debt by $4 trillion over a decade by cutting defense and discretionary spending curbing federal entitlement costs and reforming the tax code</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>it garnered a majority of votes on the presidential commission  5 democrats and 5 republicans and one independent  but not the supermajority required to send the plan to congress for a vote</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a main criticism of bowlessimpson had been that it was not written in legislative language  which is where the rubber meets the road in terms of the details of any proposal</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>now however a legislative version is circulating among some members of congress who are offering feedback bowles said</s>
<s>the original 66page report has morphed into a more than 600pagebill</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>among the campaigns core principles is that any final debtreduction plan must affect all parts of the budget preserve economic growth and protect the needy said fix the debt cochair ed rendell the former democratic governor of pennsylvania at a press conference</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the idea is to make it safe for republicans to consider raising more revenue without fear of political blowback and for democrats to consider reducing entitlement and other spending</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its necessary to create an environment where its not only good policy to sign such a plan but good politics as well said rendell who will work alongside former republican sen judd gregg the campaigns other cochair</s>
</p>
