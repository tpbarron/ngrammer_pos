<p>
<s>desperate for loans some people are posting ads on craigslist in hopes of finding cosigners</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>new york cnnmoney  in a new sign of desperation people are turning to craigslist to search for cosigners when they dont have good enough credit to qualify for loans on their own</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>javier cruz 22 who came to the united states on a student visa from mexico was hoping to transfer to texas tech universitys twoyear nursing program next fall after he finishes the year at el paso community college</s>
<s>but when his mom lost her job eight months ago the monthly checks cruz had been receiving from his parents stopped</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>without that help he couldnt afford the nursing programs $8000 per semester tuition</s>
<s>he applied for a $20000 loan but given his limited credit history and low income he didnt qualify without a cosigner</s>
<s>none of his friends had good enough credit so he turned to craigslist  offering $1000 to anyone willing to cosign on the loan</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>everybody talks about craigslist and looks for things there so i decided to put an ad there he said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related student loan basics</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>since he first posted the ad last month cruz has received four responses</s>
<s>but each respondent has either requested his bank account number or routing number or asked for the $1000 up front  leading him to believe they were scammers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>and if cruz doesnt remain a student he could lose his visa</s>
<s>if i dont get a loan i think im going to have to go back with my parents in mexico he said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>just scroll through craigslists financial services section and youll see ads from people like cruz seeking cosigners for personal auto student home and business loans  as well as cosigners for rental homes or apartments</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>one woman from montana promises $500 to a special person willing to cosign a $2500 loan that will help her purchase a 1978 shovelhead harley davidson while someone in ohio is offering up a tractor and car as collateral for anyone who cosigns a $5000 personal loan</s>
<s>a richmond vabased quichemaking company seeking a business loan is willing to give a cosigner $5000 and a stake in the company</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a craigslist spokeswoman didnt respond to requests for comment about this practice or whether its permitted on the site</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>its a dangerous trend said john ulzheimer president of consumer education at smartcreditcom</s>
<s>borrowers looking for cosigners can easily get scammed out of the money theyre offering as a payment or get their identity stolen if they provide their personal information</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the proposition is even riskier for anyone who cosigns one of these loans</s>
<s>while it might seem like an easy way to make some quick cash the ad could be a ploy to steal your identity and money</s>
<s>and there are other serious consequences that you face as a cosigner</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related your credit score isnt what you think it is</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>youre cosigning for someone a bank has already identified as someone who doesnt deserve the loan said ulzheimer</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>not only are you expected to pay the loan in full if the borrower fails to make payments or defaults but you may also be on the hook for late fees and collection costs</s>
<s>creditors can sue and attempt to garnish your wages to collect the money and late payments or any record of default will appear on your credit reports and hurt your credit scores</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the chances of being asked to step in are high as many as three out of four cosigners are called upon to repay loans that have gone into default according to the federal trade commission</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>even if the borrower and cosigner sign a legal contract that says the cosigner isnt responsible for the debt the bank can still go after them if the borrower defaults  and it is then up to the cosigner to chase down the borrower for the funds owed said carol kaplan a spokeswoman for the american bankers association</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but some people are still tempted to take this risk</s>
<s>according to larry williams a 25yearold student at rutgers university in new jersey he has come close to finding someone willing to cosign for the $8000 loan hes seeking</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>williams hasnt been able to keep up with his rent and other bills after losing several thousand dollars to gambling and other bad financial decisions</s>
<s>the loan would help him get back on track he says and he plans to find fulltime work when he graduates next spring so he can pay it off</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>after posting an ad on craigslist offering to pay a cosigner $2000 he was inundated with responses from scammers</s>
<s>he even paid a $10 fee to sign up for a matchmaking service that promised to pair him with a cosigner but it never panned out</s>
<s>eventually though he heard from two different people who agreed to help him</s>
<s>they even asked him for the cosigner form to sign</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related can i get out of a car loan that i cosigned</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>but at the last minute they each decided it was just too risky  especially since williams had no collateral</s>
<s>one of them had even lost money as a cosigner in the past he said</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>williams said he understands that its a big risk but he is still hopeful</s>
<s>i know its like strangers and everything on craigslist but i dont have any family members to go to to cosign a loan for me he said</s>
<s>i almost found success so im hoping theres just one person out there whos willing to help</s>
</p>
