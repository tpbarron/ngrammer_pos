<p>
<s>thanks for getting in touch</s>
<s>ive been keeping tabs on fiscal cliff in my capacity as chair of the new words committee of the american dialect society</s>
<s>during the societys annual meeting in the first week of january well be selecting our word of the year for 2012 and i expect fiscal cliff will be in the running  if it can hold off such contenders as gangnam style frankenstorm double down and doxing</s>
<s> yes some of these are phrases and not single words but woty candidates can be anything that could appear as a dictionary entry including compounds phrases and prefixes</s>

<p>
<s>the 1957 new york times article is the earliest appearance ive found in the newspaper databases though its a bit different since its talking about homeowners rather than budgetmakers being pushed off the fiscal cliff</s>
<s>its not until the early 1980s that fiscal cliff began to become popular as a metaphor for the precarious condition of city state and federal budgets</s>
<s>here are some examples going back to 1975</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>who hasnt looked with horror at new york citys financial plight</s>
<s>the nations biggest richest city is about to go over the fiscal cliff if the state and federal governments dont lend a helping hand</s>
<s>  mike kingston oped dallas morning news june 16 1975  analysis has schools heading off fiscal cliff again</s>
<s>  headline cleveland plain dealer jan 6 1981  should the economy worsen or congress approve all the new budget cuts proposed for the next year a number of states are sure to go over the fiscal cliff</s>
<s>  masthead editorial chicago tribune aug 1 1982  remember the battle of the california budget</s>
<s>remember the state was supposedly going over a fiscal cliff because of proposition 13 and the recession</s>
<s>  masthead editorial san diego union oct 3 1983  the process allows the city portland to alter course well before reaching the edge of a fiscal cliff</s>
<s>  larry hilderbrand oped the oregonian nov 25 1985</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>i also found a much earlier example of a similar metaphor the fiscal precipice</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the free silver shriekers are striving to tumble the united states over the same fiscal precipice</s>
<s>  unsigned editorial chicago tribune sep 9 1893</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the cliff metaphor is evocative in that it calls to mind cinematic cliffhangers going back to the silent movie era</s>
<s>and the image of driving off the fiscal cliff is now a potent one thanks to the climactic scene in thelma and louise</s>
</p>
