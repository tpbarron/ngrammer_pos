<p>
<s>beating the high cost of caskets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>with average prices ranging from $2400 for steel to $3500 wood and some copper and bronze models going for $10000 or more the casket is usually the most expensive item in a funeral</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the good news discount suppliers are growing in number from independent online sellers to retailers like costco and walmart where coffins often go for less than half the price of comparable ones sold by funeral homes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the bad news funeral homes dont make it easy for you to take your casket business elsewhere</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in some cases funeral directors may refuse to accept outside merchandise or charge a handling fee which violates the funeral rule</s>
<s>the practice resulted in one of the three enforcement actions by the ftc this year in june andrew torregrossa  sons funeral home in brooklyn agreed to pay $32000 to settle charges that it had refused services to two families one was an ftc secret shopper unless they bought a casket inhouse</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>owner andrew torregrossa called the incidents unfortunate and declined further comment</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>related the worst investing mistake</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the february issue of the trade publication funeral services insider funeral directors anonymously shared other tactics for handling customers who want to buy merchandise elsewhere</s>
<s>several suggested raising the basicservices fee to offset lower merchandise revenue</s>
<s>another favored disparaging the quality of outside vendors by saying go ahead</s>
<s>but we are offering americanmade products and not chinesemade</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the publication also recommended buying caskets from firms that create private labels it will have a different model number and the family wont know its the exact same option available at another firm</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>choosing cremation wont inure you to the casket push</s>
<s>a growing number of funeral homes offer casket rentals usually for $800 or so to view the body and expensive urns and containers for the remains</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>some also encourage buying a casket outright for the cremation even though a cardboard box is sufficient</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>directors may try to get around that with questions that imply going without a casket is less than savory says markin</s>
<s>they might say would you like a chance to say goodbye</s>
<s> when the grieving party agrees the funeral director might say well you dont want to see your mom laid out in a cardboard box</s>
<s> suddenly youre back to buying a coffin</s>
</p>

<s>related watchdogs for health care</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>gary runes a retired dentist from tiburon calif experienced this while making arrangements for his aunts cremation in 2007</s>
<s>she had prepaid the cremation fees but the funeral director assumed we would buy a coffin to put her in says runes</s>
<s>at first that was the only option offered</s>

</p>

<p>
<s>runes says he was also pitched other products such as amulets and special urns for safeguarding the ashes</s>
<s>slocum says its not uncommon to receive cremated remains in a box or a bag marked temporary container to get you to buy a pricey urn</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>what you should do</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>look outside the home</s>
<s>check out retailers like costcocom whose bestselling casket goes for $950 including shipping</s>
<s>another direct supplier with a wide selection bestprice casketscom has free ground shipping</s>
<s>most caskets can be delivered within a day</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>ask for more options</s>
<s>casket shoppers generally buy one of the first three models theyre shown</s>
<s>press for more choices and ask to see a catalogue says markin</s>
<s>also request 20gauge steel not 18gauge which is thicker and costs $700 to $1000 more</s>
<s>markin says both are suitable and no one would know the difference</s>
