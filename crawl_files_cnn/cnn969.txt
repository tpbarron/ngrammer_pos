<s>the retreat from us stocks continued last week with investors pulling $48 billion of domestic stock mutual funds</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mutual fund investors shrugged their shoulders at the federal reserves latest ploy to stimulate the sluggish economy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the first week following the feds launch of a third round of quantitative easing or qe3 investors yanked $48 billion from us stock mutual funds according to data from the investment company institute</s>
<s>that was the quickest pace of outflows since early august</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the acceleration of the exodus from stock mutual funds is curious as the feds policy aims to prop up the economy by keeping interest rates low to support the housing market and boost the stock market</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the weeks retreat brings the 2012 outflow total to nearly $87 billion  by comparison those funds lost in the neighborhood of $67 billion during the first nine months of 2010 and $83 billion during the first nine months of 2011</s>
</p>

</p>

<p>

<s> in fact gross cut the holdings of treasury bonds in the pimco total return fund pttrx leading up to the feds widelyanticipated qe3 announcement earlier this month</s>
</p>
