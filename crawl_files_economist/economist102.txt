<p>
<s>in the 1960s the md alpine a greek ship washed up on a beach in chittagong</s>
<s>locals scurried out to the vessel and pulled off what they could</s>
<s>a cheap and resilient labour force coupled with flat beaches for intentional strandings ushered in the start of a new industry for bangladesh dismantling the great hulks of the sea and selling their steel for scrap</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at its height in 2008 bangladesh’s shipbreaking industry accounted for half of all ships scrapped in the world according to ihs a consultancy</s>
<s>today the country accounts for around a fifth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in these years bangladeshi ship breakers found themselves at the forefront of criticism as ngos and pressure groups exposed some of the worst practices causing environmental and human harm</s>
<s>these included high health risks due to injuries noxious fumes and the handling of asbestos</s>
<s>critics say one way in which bangladesh competes on cost is that poor workers are unlikely to file claims for accidents or bad health</s>
<s>another advantage is or was the use of child labour</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in 2009 the bangladesh environmental lawyers association bela a publicadvocacy group convinced the supreme court to ban all ship recycling not meeting certain environmental standards</s>
<s>the court’s decision meant that by 2010 the shipbreaking industry had come to a halt</s>
<s>zahirul islam of php a local manufacturer with a big shipbreaking division the industry prefers to call it ship recycling says that for 14 months the company was unable to import a single vessel for breaking</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>knockon effects hurt the wider economy</s>
<s>a world bank study estimated that ship breaking employed over 200000 in bangladesh</s>
<s>many of the jobs were subsequently lost</s>
<s>and domestic steel prices rose sharply</s>
<s>half of all bangladesh’s steel comes from breaking ships</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>under pressure from the ship breakers bangladesh’s prime minister sheikh hasina has since relaxed the regulations</s>
<s>hefzatur rahman president of the bangladesh ship breakers association believes this has saved the industry</s>
<s>from just a score of vessels scrapped in the main part of chittagong two years ago about 150 were broken up in 2011</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>greens are not happy and want the ban reimposed</s>
<s>delphine reuter of the shipbreaking platform an ngo in brussels describes ship recycling as “close to slavery”</s>
<s>it and bela are leading the call for more regulation</s>
<s>that bothers international shipping firms and ship brokers which argue that bangladeshi ship breakers have cleaned up their act</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>at the international maritime organisation the un agency responsible for curbing shipping pollution and ensuring safety the head of pollution prevention nikos mikelis says environmentalists present bangladesh with a false choice</s>
<s>“they say they are happy to have the industry but not on the beaches</s>
<s>where do they want it</s>
<s>in the mountains”</s>
</p>
