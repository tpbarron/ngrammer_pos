<p>
<s>it is more than a century since cartographers drew east africa’s coastal strip as a single territory</s>
<s>a map from 1876 shows “zanguebar” stretching from what is now southern somalia to northern mozambique</s>
<s>in the colonial carveup that followed lines were drawn between the port cities of mombasa and dar es salaam and the island of zanzibar</s>
<s>the swahili coast named after a language created by the cohabitation of inland bantu tribes and arab traders and slavers was at various times divided between four colonial countries britain germany italy and portugal</s>
<s>their vast possessions in the hinterland eventually became kenya tanzania somalia and mozambique</s>
<s>yet in terms of culture religion and geography the coastal strip especially its swathe in the middle has retained a distinct identity</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>to that now add rising discontent</s>
<s>inequality land grabs and corruption have soured many coastal communities in recent years</s>
<s>in radical islam some now find an outlet for their anger</s>
<s>a spate of apparently unrelated church burnings riots disappearances and assassinations has swept the coast</s>
<s>secessionist sentiment is rising</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in michenzani district of stone town on the island of zanzibar which is part of tanzania angry slogans decorate mouldy concrete walls denouncing “muungano” swahili for union with the mainland</s>
<s>since october 16th rioters have repeatedly clashed with police following the brief disappearance of a popular local cleric</s>
<s>farid hadi ahmed the leader of uamsho or “awakening” which has recently evolved from a religious charity into an islamist political movement demands independence for zanzibar restrictions on alcohol consumption and a dress code for the tens of thousands of foreign tourists visiting the island every year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>political violence is not new to zanzibar nor is unease among religious conservatives over the behaviour of holidaymakers</s>
<s>but uamsho has succeeded in funnelling cultural and political tensions into support for radical islamism</s>
<s>the group denies involvement in church burning but openly feeds resentment of wabara or mainland tanzanians</s>
<s>supporters are implicated in attacks on bars said to be owned by immigrants</s>
<s>almas ali a history teacher calls the 1964 union with the mainland a “bad marriage”</s>
<s>a divorce he says is long overdue</s>
<s>grievances include the loss of tax privileges in the 1990s that hit transit trading and tanzania’s failure to join the organisation of islamic cooperation a club of muslim countries that locals hoped would channel aid to zanzibar</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>islamist hotheads used to support tanzania’s main opposition party the civic united front cuf</s>
<s>but in 2010 it formed a unity government with the ruling revolutionary party ccm following unrest amid accusations of voter fraud</s>
<s>this disappointed many zanzibari supporters and created a political vacuum on the island</s>
<s>ismail jussa the deputy cuf leader says “by the time we woke up we found ourselves engulfed by this religious group”</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>officially unemployment on the islands is 34 but officials at the zanzibar chamber of commerce say the real rate is much higher with youth joblessness and underemployment estimated at 85</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>meanwhile kenya’s main port city of mombasa erupted in august over the murder of a local cleric aboud rogo mohamed who was shot in his car by unknown assailants</s>
<s>the cleric who was barred from travelling to america for alleged links to somali militants often visited islamist forums in zanzibar</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a banned secessionist group called the mombasa republican council which has christian as well as muslim supporters won a court battle this year to get legal status</s>
<s>it blames kenya’s centralised government for coastal poverty</s>
<s>calling the group a “criminal gang” infested with jihadists the government violently cracked down three members of the council were killed when its leader omar hamisi mwamnuadzi was arrested on october 15th</s>
<s>one of kenya’s leading clerics sheikh mohammed dor an mp has since been arrested accused of seeking to fund secessionists</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>across the border tanzania’s business capital dar es salaam has been rocked by the worst religious riots in years</s>
<s>churches were looted and burned on october 12th</s>
<s>sheikh issa ponda a radical cleric has been arrested and accused of inciting violence</s>
<s>if the secessionist groups up and down the coast link up they could become a powerful dissident force</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the recent discovery of gas along the coast could make things still worse</s>
<s>mohamed hafidh khalfan an economist at the state university of zanzibar fears a nigerianstyle insurgency “poverty is like a fuel that just needs a spark to blow it up”</s>
</p>
