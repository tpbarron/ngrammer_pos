<p>
<s>for the arab world 2012 will be a year of constitutionwriting</s>
<s>leading the way will be egypt tunisia and libya</s>
<s>having toppled their dictators and held elections new governments will appoint committees to wrestle with the tricky business of drafting new constitutions</s>
<s>term limits and electoral rules will be rejigged as these countries shakily make their way towards democracy </s>
</p>

<p>
<s>defining the role of religion will prove the most contentious issue</s>
<s>despite calls from secularists and some religious minorities such as egypts copts islam will remain the official religion of the state throughout the middle east and the inspiration for legislation</s>
<s>but no islamist party will win sufficient power to push through changes imposing saudistyle sharia law </s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that will be welcome news for women in the arab world hoping that the new constitutions will improve their lot</s>
<s>the language dictating quotas for the numbers of female parliamentarians will be rewritten in egypt</s>
<s>however articles insisting that womens role in society must not interfere with their duties towards their families may be retained</s>
<s>and tunisias constitutionframers will not ditch the section that entitles women to only half as much inheritance as men</s>
<s>even with a revolution centuries of tradition are not easily overcome</s>
</p>
