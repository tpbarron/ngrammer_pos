<p>
<s>nelson mandelas successor as president of south africa thabo mbeki was often asked how he felt stepping into the great mans shoes</s>
<s>eventually he grew so irritated by the question that he quipped that mr mandela wore “such ugly shoes” that he would “never be seen dead” in them</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the new boss of apple will understand how mr mbeki felt</s>
<s>steve jobs is a hard act to follow</s>
<s>apples cofounder perhaps the most admired corporate leader in the world stood down in 2011 some six weeks before his death from cancer</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>tim cook his successor will have a rocky 2012</s>
<s>apple will remain immensely profitable thanks to its ipads and iphones</s>
<s>mr cook is competent enough not to squander mr jobss legacy</s>
<s>but to measure up to mr jobs he will need to spot the potential of products that consumers dont yet know they need</s>
<s>like mr mbeki mr cook will find that his feet are too small for the job</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>following an unsuccessful boss is not easy either</s>
<s>hewlettpackard the worlds biggest technology firm by sales is in trouble it has lost six chief executives since 1999</s>
<s>léo apotheker who was sacked in september 2011 was hoping to spin off hps lowmargin personalcomputer business and concentrate on highmargin business services</s>
<s>he failed to persuade investors that this would work</s>
<s>in 2012 his successor meg whitman who ran ebay before an unsuccessful attempt to become californias governor will try to restore hps shine</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>social media will grow in 2012 but the big rewards will accrue only to a few</s>
<s>andrew mason the young boss of groupon a firm that uses social media to sell coupons for realworld stores will be the biggest flop of 2012</s>
<s>the groupon model is easy to copy so dozens of local clones will eat mr masons discounted lunch</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mark zuckerberg will do better</s>
<s>the boyish boss of facebook will reap billions by taking his company public in 2012</s>
<s>the more people join facebook the greater the advantage of joining so the social networks success is selfreinforcing</s>
<s>mr zuckerberg will flounder however when he ventures into china in 2012</s>
<s>facebook will find chinas opaque laws and aggressive censorship tough going</s>
<s>if mr zuckerberg is not careful his china venture could damage facebooks brand in the rest of the world</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the bosses to watch in china will be robin li and jack ma</s>
<s>mr ma the founder of alibaba chinas biggest online marketplace will either split with yahoo the plodding american internet firm that was one of his early investors or buy it</s>
<s>mr li the young head of baidu chinas largest search engine will lock horns with chinas state media giants which resent both his success and the challenge he poses to their monopoly of information</s>
<s>yet mr li will thrive because his service is now so popular that the government cannot kill it without sparking unrest</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>growth in the rich world will remain sluggish in 2012 so western companies will grow principally by doing business with fastgrowing emerging markets</s>
<s>reckitt benckiser a maker of household cleaning goods has done well by selling to the emerging middle class</s>
<s>the firms new boss rakesh kapoor will use his knowledge of india to sell even more throat lozenges and detergent</s>
<s>by contrast the new boss of campbells denise morrison will not persuade chinese and indian consumers to abandon homemade soup for the canned stuff  </s>
</p>

<p>
<s>firms in emerging markets will increasingly chase business in other emerging markets rather than concentrating on their traditional markets in rich countries</s>
<s>indian outsourcing firms for example will fix software and provide backoffice support for firms in africa latin america and eastern europe</s>
<s>natarajan chandrasekaran of tata consulting services sd</s>
<s>shibulal the new boss of infosys and nv “tiger” tyagarajan the new boss of genpact will lead the charge</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>kunhee or cant he</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>several familycontrolled firms will see the baton passed to the next generation</s>
<s>in most cases keeping control within the family will be a mistake</s>
<s>james murdoch though a capable executive will not increase the clout of news corporation the way father rupert did</s>
<s>lee kunhee at samsung a south korean conglomerate is grooming two children as potential successors neither will bring fresh ideas</s>
<s>by contrast indias tata group a teatosteel conglomerate will replace ratan tata the chairman with a company insider but not a member of the tata clan</s>
<s>that will help the group become more professional as it continues its expansion outside india</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>young chief executives in the west expect not only to make money for shareholders but also to solve all manner of other problems from curbing climate change to fostering social cohesion observes jay lorsch of harvard business school</s>
<s>but the bosses with the best shot at changing the world for the better will be the ones who concentrate on business itself </s>
</p>
