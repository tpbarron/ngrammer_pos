<p>
<s>readers of bild germanys bestselling newspaper might be forgiven for thinking that a matinée idol will soon take the countrys top corporate job</s>
<s>“the cricket player with george clooney charm” is how it describes anshu jain the indian financier who will become cochief executive of deutsche bank in 2012</s>
<s>mr jain is not much less foreign to the banks culture than mr clooney himself</s>
<s>he will be the banks first nongermanspeaking chief executive</s>
<s>his background in risky investment banking a world away from deutsches staid domestic business also makes him controversial</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>a wave of new young leaders will make their mark on european business in 2012</s>
<s>they are pushing aside the old guard on the continent sometimes roughly</s>
<s>the trend is most pronounced in germany</s>
<s>as well as deutsche bank deutsche telekom henkel a consumergoods group sap a businesssoftware giant bayer pharmaceuticals and chemicals and eon a utility all have new chief executives in their 40s or early 50s</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>germany incs new leadership will be far more international than before</s>
<s>of the eight new bosses at the six companies mentioned above sap like deutsche bank has appointed cochiefs only three are german</s>
<s>appointing foreigners has not always been easy</s>
<s>peter terium a 48yearold dutchman will take over in 2012 at rwe group germanys biggest power producer after facing an internal row over the succession his rival candidate was older and german</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in italy business leaders under 70 are considered mere striplings</s>
<s>but there are some signs of change</s>
<s>in 2011 alberto nagel the 46yearold chief executive of mediobanca an investment bank at the heart of corporate italy led the ouster from generali an insurance company of its 76yearold chairman cesare geronzi</s>
<s>mr geronzi was later sentenced to four years in prison for his role in a bankruptcy</s>
<s>mr nagel has improved transparency and governance at mediobanca and has imposed an age limit for top managers of 65</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in spain an important handover came in 2011 when amancio ortega the founder of inditex a fashion and logistics group which is one of the countrys most innovative firms handed the chairmanship to pablo isla its 47yearold chief executive</s>
<s>frances most disruptive young businessman is xavier niel a 44yearold entrepreneur who is the countrys first internet billionaire</s>
<s>in 2012 he will start a longawaited battle against the countrys three dominant telecoms firms</s>
<s>iliad his company will start selling mobile telephony for around half the price charged by the incumbents</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the coming year will also shine a spotlight on the young heirs to europes business dynasties</s>
<s>arnaud lagardère the son of the founder of lagardère a french mediatoweapons group plans to take over the chairmanship of eads europes defence and aerospace champion in 2012</s>
<s>some at the company however reckon he is not serious enough for the job</s>
<s>he scandalised french business when he released an online video of himself smooching with a new girlfriend</s>
<s>antoine arnault son of bernard arnault a french financier and luxury mogul will launch the first readytowear collection for berluti a maker of mens luxury shoes</s>
<s>success in the new job he was formerly at louis vuitton a handbagandluggage maker could pave his way to the top of lvmh a luxury group</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>businesswomen will be in great demand in 2012</s>
<s>viviane reding the eus justice commissioner has threatened to introduce compulsory quotas for female board members if european firms dont bring in more women by march</s>
<s>but the real question is whether they will move beyond tokenism and promote women to run things not just sit on the board</s>
<s>one female executive to watch is pascale sourisse who runs the defenceandsecuritysystems unit at thales a french aerospace and ­defence group</s>
<s>she nearly became one of europes most prominent businesswomen in 2011 when frances government considered her as a replacement for anne lauvergeon as boss of areva a nuclearenergy champion in the end it chose a man</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>several of germanys familycontrolled firms are promoting daughters who have grown up in the business</s>
<s>henkel recently became the first of germanys big bluechip firms to have a female head of the supervisory board</s>
<s>simone bageltrah is a greatgreatgranddaughter of henkels founder</s>
<s>young often foreign and occasionally female these will be the new bosses to watch in europe in 2012 </s>
</p>
