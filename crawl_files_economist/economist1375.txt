<p>
<s>even if you still have to think twice about the meaning of “giga” and “tera” in computerspeak youd better get ready for “peta” “exa” and “zetta”</s>
<s>these binary prefixes which denote 1m 1 billion and 1 trillion gigabytes respectively will be used more and more often the amount of digital data is exploding with a staggering 18 zettabytes in 2011 up from 12 zettabytes in the previous year</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this deluge has long been a topic among geeks 2012 will be the year in which the big data trend gets noticed beyond their ranks</s>
<s>many more firms will start to analyse huge piles of data to optimise ­everything from their supply chains to their customer relationships</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in the past because data storage was expensive lots had to be thrown away</s>
<s>information was locked up in computer systems and could not be combined with other sources</s>
<s>even if firms were able to aggregate their data in one place called a “data warehouse” and sift through the information it was often already out of date</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>all this is changing—rapidly</s>
<s>the price for storage is plunging by 2020 storing a petabyte will cost a mere $4 predicts forrester a marketresearch firm</s>
<s>software to handle such huge amounts of data is improving too</s>
<s>hadoop a new type of database can be used to sift through big data streams in real time—and not just orderly numbers but also “unstructured” data for instance any kind of text</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in a world oozing data sensors measure everything from speed to smell</s>
<s>smartphone applications generate vast quantities of “data exhaust” information that is produced when users engage in say skiing and thanks to a motion sensor the phone can tell whether somebody has just had a bad fall</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>social media are adding to the flood the number of messages on twitter will exceed 500m per day by the end of 2012</s>
<s>by opening their data vaults some ­national and local governments are doing the same</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>most important firms will discover that they can extract value from the data</s>
<s>a study by the mckinsey global institute a thinktank found that analysing healthcare data could yield $300 billionworth of savings in america alone</s>
<s>one of the pioneers in this field is britains national institute for health and clinical excellence which uses large datasets to investigate the cost and benefit of new drugs and existing expensive treatments</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>firms will also use data for new business models</s>
<s>rollsroyce no longer needs to sell all its jet engines it can charge for their use</s>
<s>by continuously assessing their performance it can predict when engines are more likely to fail so that customers can schedule engine changes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>already the number of datadriven startups is growing—particularly when it comes to social media</s>
<s>lexalytics for instance analyses the sentiment of utterings on twitter facebook and other such services</s>
<s>klout measures the influence of socialmedia users and some firms give people with a high klout score preferential treatment</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>for big data to become huge however there are still hurdles to leap</s>
<s>for one thing the tools to analyse data are not yet good enough</s>
<s>and people with the skills to analyse data are scarce and will become scarcer</s>
<s>by 2018 there will be a “talent gap” of between 140000 and 190000 people says the mc­kinsey global institute</s>
<s>the main problem may be privacy</s>
<s>it is unlikely that people will want to live in a “yotta world” 1000 trillion gigabytes in which their every move is instantly digitised and added to the flood of public data </s>
</p>

<p>
