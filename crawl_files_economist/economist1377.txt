<s>it may get off to a slow start since it has not yet signed up many cable and satellite distributors</s>
<s>but its creation points to one of the media businesss few bright spots</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>having fallen steeply after the collapse of lehman brothers in september 2008 the shares of all the big american media companies have outperformed the market since march</s>
<s>but recession has struck some parts of the industry much harder than others changing its shape</s>
<s>as a rule media products that are sold in shops—cds dvds and magazines—have suffered</s>
<s>advertising is showing only tentative signs of recovery</s>
<s>the kind of media for which people pay a monthly bill in contrast has not only held up better but has in some instances prospered through the downturn</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>cable and satellite television was a good business going into the recession and is now triumphant</s>
<s>in the year to june 30th britains bskyb added more subscribers obtained more revenue from each customer and reported more profit than the year before</s>
<s>discovery communications which derives almost all of its revenue from cable notched up a 13 increase in profits in the second quarter</s>
<s>in the past year the fortunes of big media groups have depended largely on the proportion of their revenues coming from pay television</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>cable networks obtain about half of their revenues from advertising and half from carriage fees paid by the firms that distribute their channels which in turn get paid by subscribers</s>
<s>in the past year increases in carriage fees have outpaced inflation offsetting weakness in advertising</s>
<s>at time warners cable networks for example advertising fell by $30m in the second quarter compared with a year earlier</s>
<s>income from distribution rose by $144m</s>
<s>“people would sooner unplug their refrigerators than their cable boxes” says craig moffett an analyst at sanford bernstein</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>as pay television has soared and just about everything else has fallen even the most diversified conglomerates accounts have been transformed see chart</s>
<s>news corporations cable channels are worth more than broadcast television film and newspapers put together</s>
<s>although an advertising recovery will rebalance such firms somewhat the underlying trend is clear</s>
<s>media firms are investing in paytelevision markets in latin america eastern europe and asia which can be expected to grow</s>
<s>the number of channels in emerging markets is rising so fast it is actually boosting the firms that own the satellites see article</s>
<s>viewers and creative verve are drifting steadily from broadcast to cable networks</s>
<s>on october 5th disney appointed rich ross who ran its cable channels worldwide to head its film studio—an acknowledgment of their success in producing lucrative new content</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the strength of subscription television has encouraged media firms to try charging for other products</s>
<s>disney recently began selling subscriptions to its large online library of childrens books</s>
<s>viacoms chief executive philippe dauman said last month that the company was exploring ways of getting people to subscribe to its popular online games based on characters such as dora the explorer</s>
<s>the firms controlling shareholder sumner redstone once observed that content is king</s>
<s>but at the moment subscription is king</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>there has also been much talk of creating new subscription models for newspapers and magazines</s>
<s>as far as their online offerings go this is still mostly talk</s>
<s>the boldest conglomerate is news corporation which sells online subscriptions to the wall street journal and will begin charging for the newspapers smartphone applications</s>
<s>this week the firm announced plans to charge for membership of rewards schemes run by britains times and sunday times</s>
<s>more quietly but just as profoundly many newspapers and magazines are stepping up their homedelivery efforts as newsstand sales falter</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the best model in media has its limits however</s>
<s>the fact that nearly all newspaper websites remain free suggests how hard it is to charge for content that has been commoditised</s>
<s>successful subscription models for music have proved elusive for a similar reason</s>
<s>spotify a musicstreaming service created by swedish programmers that has grown at an astonishing rate is trying to move from dependence on advertising to subscriptions</s>
<s>whether it succeeds will depend partly on the record labels</s>
<s>at the moment they view such upstarts as a handy way of weaning customers from illegal filesharing websites</s>
<s>if they come to view them as competition for cd sales and digital downloads the musicstreaming sites are in trouble</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>even the mighty paytelevision business is showing signs of strain</s>
<s>cable and satellite operators have tolerated the shrinking margins that come with higher carriage fees so far but will not do so for ever</s>
<s>yet they may find it hard to pass price increases on to customers</s>
<s>mr moffett reckons the finances of the poorest 40 of american households have been so stretched by the recession that they have little money left for entertainment</s>
<s>unemployment in california has reached 12 in michigan it stands at 15</s>
<s>it may be that people will have to unplug both their refrigerators and their cable boxes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>then there is the looming threat of the internet with its tendency to disintermediate content from carrier</s>
<s>consumers can already obtain many broadcasttelevision programmes online and the worry is that they will eventually drop their cable and satellite services</s>
<s>jeff bewkes the head of time warner is leading a charge to prevent that</s>
<s>he envisages an authentication system that could be used to restrict access to some online content to those who subscribe to multichannel television</s>
<s>the fear of “cordcutting” may also underlie comcasts ambitious and given the dismal history of media mergers risky attempt to acquire a controlling stake in nbc universal a content company now owned by general electric and vivendi</s>

<p>
