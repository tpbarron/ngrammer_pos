<p>
<s>after a decade of bungabunga and a year of financial turmoil corporate italy is feeling sick</s>
<s>but a crisis for some is an opportunity for others</s>
<s>the past year has been a great time to buy italian companies</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>two factors make them attractively cheap</s>
<s>one is the weakness of the euro which has lost 15 against the chinese yuan 19 against the japanese yen and 9 against the dollar since the start of 2010</s>
<s>the other is the nearparalysis of italys domestic banking system</s>
<s>rickety banks are reluctant to lend to local firms</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>cashstrapped firms that cannot borrow often try to raise equity instead</s>
<s>many are looking outside italy</s>
<s>foreign direct investment has soared</s>
<s>from the last quarter of 2009 to the third quarter of 2010 italy attracted only $796m</s>
<s>the next year the inflow jumped eightfold to $66 billion</s>
<s>the takeovers by french firms of bulgari a luxurygoods firm and parmalat a dairy group accounted for much of the rise</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>cheap luxury is often the lure</s>
<s>italy is packed with financially precarious firms that make desirable products with glamorous brand names</s>
<s>some also boast superb technology</s>
<s>for chinese buyers this is irresistible</s>
<s>many chinese firms have cash but few have global brands</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>de tomaso a maker of sporty cars has been in financial trouble for years</s>
<s>hotyork investment a chinese group senses a bargain</s>
<s>in january it said it would buy 80 of de tomaso for €60m $79m</s>
<s>hotyork reckons cars made with sexy italian design technology and craftsmanship will wow chinese drivers</s>
<s>it plans to invest another €400m or so to boost production near turin and market the cars abroad</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>hotyork is not alone</s>
<s>earlier this year shandong heavy industry group of china bought 75 of ferretti a builder of luxury yachts that had previously bounced between banks and privateequity funds</s>
<s>in december eland world a south korean retailer bought 49 of coccinelle a firm that makes fashionable handbags for €155m coccinelles parent firm mariella burani fashion group went bust in 2010</s>
<s>italy is famous for food as well as luxury in january princes a british subsidiary of japans mitsubishi corporation said it would buy 51 of a giant tomatoprocessing business in southern italy</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>italy has not historically been a magnet for crossborder investment</s>
<s>emma marcegaglia the president of confindustria italys bosses club blames red tape slow justice high taxes and a rigid labour market</s>
<s>however the perception of italy has improved recently the euro crisis has cooled a bit and the arrival in november of a technocratic government led by mario monti a former european commissioner promising lots of economic reforms has soothed investors collywobbles</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>many italian firms are familyowned and about as keen to sell up as their 30yearold sons are to leave home</s>
<s>but some of the more ambitious ones realise that if they want to expand they must look abroad for capital</s>
<s>gancia a wine and vermouth firm with burdensome debts of €35m sold a 70 stake to russian standard corporation a moscowbased firm whose business is a rum cocktail of drinks and financial services last december</s>
<s>the gancia brand is popular in italy but faces slim margins and weak growth</s>
<s>paolo fontana the firms managing director says he wants to see exports increase from 25 to 80 of sales</s>
<s>russian control should boost sales in russia and could help relaunch gancia in america britain and germany where it was strong 40 years ago</s>
