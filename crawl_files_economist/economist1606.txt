<p>
<s>after years of dizzying ascents a big dose of gravity has hit residentialproperty markets around the world</s>
<s>according to the economist’s latest roundup yearonyear prices are now falling in 12 of the 21 countries we track in five of the other nine prices are rising at a slower rate than they were a year ago</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>earthbound prices are returning many markets to “fair value” defined as the longrun average ratio of house prices to disposable income and to rents</s>
<s>housing is now around or below its fair value in eight countries</s>
<s>but reaching this mark does not mean prices will stop falling</s>
<s>after dropping by a third from their 2006 peak prices in america now stand at 19 below fair value</s>
<s>the bottom of the market is close however</s>
<s>the monthonmonth caseshiller index of 20 cities increased for the fourth consecutive time in may by 09</s>
<s>housing sales are picking up although they remain below their longrun average and the number of mortgages in foreclosure has fallen to its lowest level for three years</s>
<s>financing is cheap too real 30year fixed mortgage rates are at 30year lows</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>other markets are still in free fall</s>
<s>property prices in ireland at the foot of our table since april 2010 continue to plummet</s>
<s>they have now halved in value after a fivefold rise between 1995 and their 2007 peak</s>
<s>the pace of decline in spain a fellow eurozone sufferer quickened in the second quarter</s>
<s>although prices have already fallen by 23 from their peak they remain well above fair value and the dire state of the spanish economy where a quarter of the workforce is unemployed suggests that prices will keep diving</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>such drops would be more precipitous still were it not for the cushioning effects of ultralow interest rates on european mortgageholders</s>
<s>prices in britain fell by 07 in july compared with the previous month taking the total fall since the market peak to a rather modest 131</s>
<s>with many lenders hanging back sales remain subdued at around half their 2007 level</s>
<s>the market is heavily reliant on london and the southeast 47 of residential transactions took place in this part of the country in 2011</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>oncewild asian markets are also muted</s>
<s>prices in hong kong are now rising at a manageable 6 a year as opposed to 28 a year just 12 months ago</s>
<s>price rises in singapore have slowed in recent months too</s>
<s>our index of chinese prices fell year on year for the fifth month in a row in june</s>
<s>that may not last however prices of new homes rose month on month in 25 of the 70 cities tracked and there is plenty of room for growth</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>indeed so subdued is residential property at the moment that the list of the world’s bounciest housing markets has an unusually germanic flavour</s>
<s>austrian houseprice rises are the only ones in double digits the swiss market sits in fourth place</s>
<s>as for germany itself prices there have increased by a groundbreaking 57 over the past two years after nearly two decades of stagnation</s>
<s>hopes that a german property boom will unleash spending are slight however</s>
<s>german regulators are watchful and owneroccupation in the country stands at just 46 so any rise in prices has a fainter “wealth effect” than in britain say where 66 of homes are owneroccupied</s>
