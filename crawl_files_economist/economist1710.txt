<p>
<s>british special forces swept into the northern nigerian city of sokoto on march 8th to free a briton and an italian held by islamic extremists for ten months</s>
<s>the mission failed leaving the hostages dead and nigeria with a growing sense of doom</s>

<s>they amplify demands for strict adherence to sharia law with bombings that have killed more than 1000 people</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>yet the turmoil caused by boko haram is only the most visible of a cluster of troubles in one of africas most remote regions</s>
<s>once a playground for adventurous tourists the arid lands south of the sahara desert known as the sahel are fast becoming a nogo zone</s>
<s>chad mali and niger are the countries most affected but the convergence of jihadist extremism a sudden influx of heavy weapons and a severe drought threatens the wider region</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>low precipitation may seem normal near the sahara</s>
<s>in fact much of the sahel normally gets enough rain to allow modest farming</s>
<s>but a rise in water temperatures in the nearby gulf of guinea has shifted the flow of rain clouds southwards meteorologists say</s>

<s>longterm overgrazing and fast population growth have made the problem worse</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>oxfam an aid agency warns of a humanitarian disaster with more than 1m children facing severe malnutrition</s>
<s>villagers in chad already dig up ant hills to gather grain the ants have stored</s>
<s>but the worstaffected place is now niger a landlocked country of 15m people which even in normal times accounts for a sixth of global child deaths from malnutrition</s>
<s>save the children another aid agency says that the situation in niger has worsened since september when a lack of rain led to crop failures of up to 80</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>misery has made the sahels thousands of unemployed an easy target for recruiters from extremist groups</s>
<s>their main base lies across nigers badly patrolled border with algeria where the sahel becomes outright desert</s>
<s>a twodecadeold islamist insurgency there has adopted the mantle of global jihad and renamed itself alqaeda in the islamic maghreb</s>
<s>having failed to dislodge the military regime along algerias densely populated mediterranean coast these extremists are increasingly focused on the sandy hinterland</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in january they kidnapped a provincial governor near nigers border with libya</s>
<s>they also hold at least 18 europeans hostage</s>
<s>several of these are in the custody of a new splinter group that announced itself in december</s>

<s>to set themselves apart they strive to be even more radical</s>
<s>modern weapons flow to them from libya</s>

<p>
<s>the libyan arms bazaar particularly affects mali a country the size of california and texas combined and with a history of unrest</s>

<p>

<p>
