<p>
<s>hot and high in the sierra madre the city of saltillo is a long way from wall street</s>
<s>stuffed goats keep an eye on customers in the highstreet vaquera or cowboy outfitter where workers from the local car factories blow their pesos on snakeskin boots and $100 stetsons</s>
<s>pinstriped suits and silk ties are outnumbered by checked shirts and silver beltbuckles pickups are prized over porsches</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the financial crisis of 2008 began on the trading floors of manhattan but the biggest tremors were felt in the desert south of the rio grande</s>
<s>mexico suffered the steepest recession of any country in the americas bar a couple of caribbean tiddlers</s>
<s>its economy shrank by 61 in 2009 see chart 1</s>
<s>between the third quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009 700000 jobs were lost 260000 of them in manufacturing</s>
<s>the slump was deepest in the prosperous north worst hit was the border state of coahuila</s>
<s>saltillo its capital had grown rich exporting to america</s>
<s>the states output fell by 123 in 2009 as orders dried up</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the recession turned a reasonable decade for mexicos economy into a dreary one</s>
<s>in the ten years to 2010 income per person grew by 06 a year one of the lowest rates in the world</s>
<s>in the early 2000s mexico boasted latin americas biggest economy measured at market exchange rates but it was soon overtaken by brazil whose gdp is now twice as big and still pulling away boosted by the soaring real</s>
<s>soon brazil will take the lead in oil production which mexico has allowed to dwindle</s>
<s>as brazilians construct stadiums for the 2014 world cup and the 2016 olympics mexicans who last year celebrated the bicentenary of their independence from spain are building monuments to their past and finishing them late</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mexicos muscles</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>yet mexicos economy is packed with potential</s>
<s>thanks to the north american freetrade agreement nafta and a string of bilateral deals it trades more than argentina and brazil combined and more per person than china</s>
<s>last year it did $400 billion of business with the united states more than any country bar canada and china</s>
<s>the investment rate at more than a fifth of gdp is well ahead of brazils</s>
<s>income per person slipped below brazils in 2009 but only because of the reals surge and the pesos weakness</s>
<s>after accounting for purchasing power mexicans are still better off than brazilians</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>though expatriates whinge about bureaucracy the world bank ranks mexico the easiest place in latin america to do business and the 35theasiest in the world ahead of italy and spain</s>
<s>in brazil placed 127th companies spend 2600 hours a year filing taxes six times more than in mexico</s>
<s>registering a business takes nine days in mexico and 26 in argentina</s>
<s>the working hours of supposedly siestaloving mexicans are among the longest in the world</s>
<s>and although mexicos schools are the worst in mainly rich oecd countries they are the least bad in latin america apart from chiles</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>these strengths have helped mexico to rebound smartly from its calamitous slump</s>
<s>last year the economy grew by 54 recovering much of the ground lost in 2009</s>
<s>exports to the united states having fallen by a fifth have reached a record high</s>
<s>in the desert there are signs of life saltillos high street where four out of ten shops closed during the recession is busy again</s>
<s>cifunsa a foundry that turns out some 400000 tonnes of cast iron a year for customers such as ford and volkswagen shed 40 of its staff in 2009 but has rehired most of them and is producing more than it did before the slump</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>however the jobs market has yet to return to its prerecession state</s>
<s>nationally the official unemployment rate is 54 having peaked at 64 in 2009</s>
<s>javier lozano mexicos labour secretary believes that the prerecession mark of 41 will not be matched within the term of this government or the next ie before 2018</s>
<s>whats more the new jobs are not as good as those that were lost</s>
<s>average pay last year was 5 lower than in 2008</s>
<s>because of this and rising food prices more mexicans have slipped into poverty last year 462 of them were below the official poverty line earning less than 2114 pesos or $167 per month up from 445 in 2008</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>just as recession came from the gringos recovery depends partly on them</s>
<s>many analysts who once predicted economic growth of 5 this year cut their forecasts to under 4 after a downward revision of american gdp in july</s>
<s>exports account for nearly a third of mexicos trilliondollar gdp and most go to the united states</s>
<s>remittances provide $190 per person per year down from $240 in 2007</s>
<s>now america faces several years of lacklustre growth which poses a dilemma for mexico</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>some look at the recent explosive growth of brazil and wonder if it is time to follow its example and look to new markets</s>
<s>in 2009 only 3 of mexicos exports went to brazil russia india or china whereas brazil sent 16 of its exports to its fellow brics</s>
<s>industrialised countries receive less than half of brazils exports but 90 of mexicos</s>
<s>the interamerican development bank the biggest lender in the region describes a “two speed” latin america in which economies such as mexico which do most of their trade with developed countries lag behind those such as brazil that have forged links with emerging markets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>south or north</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>mexico has already diversified its exports</s>
<s>americas share of them has fallen from 89 in 2000 to perhaps 78 this year and will fall further according to miguel messmacher head of economic planning at mexicos finance ministry</s>
<s>sales to latin america and asia are growing twice as fast as those to america</s>
<s>the automotive industry mexicos biggest exporter is ahead of the trend though exports to america continue to rise they now make up only 65 of the total</s>
<s>eduardo solís head of the industrys national association says he would like to get the figure down to 50 by focusing on latin america and europe</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>others say mexicos economic future will always be to the north</s>
<s>“we cant just become a commodity exporter and start sending soy beans to china” says jorge castañeda a former foreign secretary</s>
<s>history geography and natural resources have wedded mexico to its wealthy neighbour “its not something we chose” he says</s>
<s>if the american economy is growing slowly mexico will just have to get a bigger chunk of it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>that task has been made harder by china</s>
<s>since china joined the world trade organisation in 2001 its share of american imports has grown fast and is now the biggest</s>
<s>the shares of canada and especially japan have fallen</s>
<s>mexicos share which almost doubled in the seven years after nafta came into effect slipped after 2001</s>
<s>but it is edging up again see chart 2</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>chinas low wages which lured factories away from mexico are rising rapidly</s>
<s>in 2003 mexican pay was three times chinese rates but now it is only 20 higher mr messmacher says</s>
<s>the rising yuan and the cheap peso accentuate this trend</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>proximity to america mexicos trump card has been made more valuable by the high oil price</s>
<s>the resolution in july of a long dispute has allowed mexican lorries to make deliveries in america which the mexican government reckons will reduce firms shipping costs by 15</s>
<s>the rise of china may also help mexico too by forcing american companies to compete more keenly</s>
<s>detroit carmakers cannot export cars to south korea but a mexican factory using american parts can notes luis de la calle a former trade minister</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>luring foreign investors has been made trickier by a spike in violence</s>
<s>since 2007 a crackdown on organised crime has caused mexicos drugtrafficking “cartels” as they are known though they are in fact rather competitive to splinter and fight</s>
<s>last year the murder rate was 17 per 100000 people a little lower than brazils but more than twothirds up on 2007</s>
<s>ernesto cordero the finance minister has estimated that the violence knocks about a percentage point off mexicos annual growth rate</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the fighting is highly concentrated last year 70 of mafiarelated killings took place in 3 of the countrys municipalities</s>
<s>in yucatán state where tourists scramble around mayan ruins the murder rate is no higher than in belgium</s>
<s>last july was the busiest ever for mexicos foreigntourist trade but there are signs that the drip of bloody stories is starting to hurt bookings</s>
<s>in the first five months of this year arrivals were 36 lower than last</s>
<s>acapulco which caters mainly to domestic tourists has virtually emptied thanks to frequent shootings in the heart of the hotel zone</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>many of the roughest areas are in the north where foreign investment is concentrated</s>
<s>in ciudad juárez a centre of maquila factories that assemble products for export the murder rate has climbed to one of the highest in the world as the sinaloa and juárez cartels battle for control of the border crossing little restrained and often aided by the local police</s>
<s>in tamaulipas a border state where violence surged last year the unemployment rate has risen to 75 the highest in the country</s>
<s>the head of a mexican multinational with operations there found recently that his local manager had been siphoning company money to the cartels</s>
<s>many rich businessmen have moved their families to america the governor of one border state is rumoured to have done the same his office denies it</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>investors have largely held their nerve</s>
<s>foreign direct investment which reached $30 billion in 2007 but fell to half that in 2009 is expected to recover to $20 billion this year</s>
<s>businessmen play down the violence mr solís admits that some car transporters have been robbed on highways but says that this year has been better than last</s>
<s>this month honda became the latest carmaker to announce plans to expand in mexico in spite of the insecurity</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>still insecurity adds costs and delays</s>
<s>the road from saltillo to monterrey the nearest big airport has become dicey so more people rely on saltillos own tiny airport where a single airline offers flights to mexico city for upwards of $400</s>
<s>conferences concerts and sporting fixtures have been cancelled in monterrey</s>
<s>in coahuila on august 20th a football match was abandoned after shots were fired outside the stadium</s>
<s>some foreign companies are even nervous about sending executives to mexico city although it has a lower murder rate than many american cities</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>from uncle sam to uncle slim</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>despite mexicos difficulties one of its citizens is the richest person in the world</s>
<s>carlos slim the son of a lebanese immigrant has made a fortune estimated by forbes at $74 billion</s>
<s>the magazine reckons that last year his net worth rose by $205 billion</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>nearly twothirds of mr slims wealth is thought to lie in américa móvil the biggest or secondbiggest mobilephone operator everywhere in latin america except chile where it is third</s>
<s>in mexico mr slims grip is particularly strong with 70 of the cellular market and 80 of landlines</s>
<s>in half the countrys 400 local areas only his company has the infrastructure to put through calls to landlines</s>
<s>not surprisingly after accounting for purchasing power home landlines in mexico cost 45 more than the oecd average and business lines 63 more see chart 3</s>
<s>mobiles are better value particularly for those who do not make many calls</s>
<s>but basic broadband access costs nearly ten times more per megabit per second of advertised speed than in the rest of the oecd</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>telecoms is not the only monopolised sector</s>
<s>a study by the oecd and mexicos federal competition commission cfc found that 31 of mexican household spending went on products supplied in monopolistic or highly oligopolistic markets</s>
<s>the poorest tenth suffered most 38 of their expenditure going on such things</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the cost of these captive markets is ruinous</s>
<s>until recently for example firms selling generic medicines were required by law to operate a plant in mexico</s>
<s>this along with a system that allows doctors to prescribe medicines by brand rather than by generic compound means that the market is dominated by expensive brands</s>
<s>generics account for less than 17 of the drugs market against 665 in america</s>
<s>medicine is a third pricier than in britain</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the labyrinth of torpitude</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>transport is expensive too</s>
<s>the handful of budget airlines that arrived in the past decade have struggled to get takeoff and landing slots at mexico citys airport which are dished out by a committee dominated by incumbents</s>
<s>the cfc found that flights to and from mexico city were between 40 and 80 dearer than those to less strangled airports</s>
<s>intercity bus routes are dominated by four firms that have divided up the country</s>
<s>fares are 10 higher than they ought to be the cfc estimates</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>banking is similarly uncompetitive</s>
<s>two banks control almost half the market for deposit accounts and twothirds of the credit and debitcard markets</s>
<s>the lack of choice means that 95 of accountholders have never switched banks</s>
<s>top of the list of saltillo businesses complaints is the scarcity and cost of credit</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>some of these pinch points are being addressed</s>
<s>the collapse last year of mexicana north americas oldest airline has presented an opportunity to auction landing slots to nimbler competitors</s>
<s>drugs should get cheaper thanks to an auction system devised by the cfc for mexicos socialsecurity institute</s>
<s>in april a new competition law introduced penalties of up to ten years in jail for collusion and empowered the cfc to make surprise inspections</s>
<s>the same month it fined mr slims mobilephone operator a record $1 billion for abusing its market dominance</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>banking has been opened to entrants such as walmart which has already shaken up mexican retailing</s>
<s>commercial credit is expanding it stands at 19 of gdp nearly double the ratio in 2003</s>
<s>lending is still less than half of what it was before the banking crisis of 1994 suggesting plenty of room for growth—certainly more than in brazil where credit already equals about half of gdp</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>forcing competition on cosy industries is still not easy</s>
<s>when the government decided in 2009 to shut down luz y fuerza a staterun electricity company that was costing the taxpayer $3 billion a year it required 1000 police in riot gear to occupy the firms offices</s>
<s>since luz y fuerza shut the wait for new connections in mexico city has fallen from ten months to four</s>
<s>but its exemployees still bring parts of the capital to a halt with protests</s>
<s>labourreform efforts to ease hiring and firing and allow sixmonth trial contracts have met opposition in congress</s>
<s>even with the new competition law few people fancy the authorities chances against mr slims lawyers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the answer is to open the economy and let foreign competition force mexican firms to adapt believes mr de la calle</s>
<s>“if you have free trade you dont need structural reforms because the companies have to compete” he says</s>
<s>he cites the pork industry which used to be blighted with hog cholera</s>
<s>farmers resisted pressure to eradicate it preferring to sell low volumes at high prices</s>
<s>when tariffs were dropped cheap pork from america forced mexican farmers to clean up their act</s>
<s>cholera was eliminated output rose and prices fell</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>other industries are ripe for similar treatment</s>
<s>oil is a prime candidate</s>
<s>pemex a state monopoly handles everything from exploration to petrol pumps</s>
<s>its profits contribute a third of government revenue allowing mexico to maintain a generous and feebly enforced tax regime</s>
<s>but decades of underinvestment have hurt production which fell from 34m barrels a day in 2004 to 26m</s>
<s>brazil which has allowed foreign investment in its oilfields is producing around 2m barrels a day and expects to be pumping 6m by 2020</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>pemexs output has stabilised in the past year and this month it awarded its first performancebased contracts a precursor to getting oil majors to explore the deep waters of the gulf of mexico</s>
<s>but efforts to make the company more efficient have been vetoed by the oil workers union</s>
<s>refineries are poorly run petrol stations forbid selfservice</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the mexican institute for competitiveness a thinktank estimates that the gdp growth rate could be raised by 25 percentage points if the oil industry were opened up and labour and competition laws reformed</s>
<s>reeling from an americanmade recession however mexico is hardly in the mood for a more open economy</s>
<s>with a presidential election next year it would be easier to keep puttering along in the shadow of brazil an economy which in some ways mexico outclasses</s>
<s>mexicos rebound from slump and its resilience to lawlessness show its underlying strength</s>
<s>if it could only bust the monopolistic dams that have parched its economy its desert might one day start to bloom</s>
</p>
