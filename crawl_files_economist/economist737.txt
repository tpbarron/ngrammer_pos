<p>
<s>business will be easier in 2010 though that is not saying much</s>
<s>companies have suffered a long and ferocious beating</s>
<s>many have expired</s>
<s>but the tougher ones will emerge leaner and stronger and ready to seize the opportunities that a gradual recovery will offer</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>america was the first to stumble and it will be among the first to pick itself up</s>
<s>after a painful bout of costcutting and jobshedding executives in the united states are confident that business will soon start to recover according to a survey by mckinsey a consultancy</s>
<s>asians are optimistic too especially in china</s>
<s>firms in the euro zone are the gloomiest</s>
<s>the global recovery will not be quick however</s>
<s>there is still a risk of a doubledip recession</s>
<s>and it is clear that business will not be the same after the crisis as it was before</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>firms will be warier of leverage</s>
<s>those that borrowed too much will suffer and possibly die</s>
<s>those with stronger balancesheets will gobble up their rivals or seduce their customers</s>
<s>entrepreneurial firms will displace market leaders as they have in past downturns</s>
<s>and firms will boost their profits the oldfashioned way by becoming more productive</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the trend towards bigger government and more regulation will continue especially in america where public spending as a share of gdp is growing fast</s>
<s>the united states government will still prop up and meddle in the financial sector stabilising the system but also cramping innovation</s>
<s>federal fingers will remain on the steering wheel in detroit</s>
<s>and government will become the biggest customer for many industries</s>
<s>more companies will open offices in washington dc to be close to the action</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the united states congress will institute a capandtrade system for curbing carbon emissions thus making it more likely that china and india will follow suit</s>
<s>but the toughest curbs will be pushed several years into the future</s>
<s>so energy prices will rise only slowly at first</s>
<s>but american firms will anticipate future price increases and scramble to adapt</s>
<s>energy consumers will strive for greater efficiency</s>
<s>producers of wind solar and other alternative sources of power will invest heavily</s>
<s>subsidies may spur the construction of new nuclear plants</s>
<s>engineering informationtechnology and consulting firms such as ge ibm and accenture will work hard to develop smart electricity grids at home and elsewhere</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>healthcare reform in america will have both domestic and global consequences</s>
<s>the government will make sure that more americans are insured and it will regulate private insurance more tightly</s>
<s>to restrain costs it will squeeze not only hospitals but also drug firms and suppliers of medical devices</s>
<s>the returns from innovation in medical technology will decrease affecting inventive firms throughout the world that rely on the american market to recoup their research costs</s>
<s>public funding for research will only partly compensate for this</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>consumption patterns will change in 2010</s>
<s>america will remain the biggest economy by far</s>
<s>but overstretched american consumers will no longer drive global growth as strongly</s>
<s>firms bent on expansion will increasingly look towards emerging markets such as china india and brazil</s>
<s>chinese productivity growth actually accelerated between 2008 and 2009 to a startling 9</s>
<s>russia will seem less inviting despite its hydrocarbon wealth because of fears about the rule of law</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>most consumers will still be short of cash so firms that make cheap stuff well will thrive</s>
<s>luxurygoods firms will flounder</s>
<s>purveyors of basic necessities such as walmart and procter  gamble will do well</s>
<s>people dont stop brushing their teeth just because there is a recession notes john quelch of harvard business school</s>
<s>asian firms that make cheap cars and washing machines for asias emerging middle class such as tata hyundai and haier will make inroads into western markets</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>innovation which slowed only slightly during the recession will accelerate again</s>
<s>firms that continued to invest in research during bad times such as google intel and toyota will reap rewards</s>
<s>america will remain the worlds biggest innovator but many asian firms will draw closer</s>
<s>manufacturers will sell more goods directly to consumers over the internet bypassing traditional retailers</s>
<s>dataintensive businesses will move more of their computing into the cloud</s>
<s>socialmedia firms such as facebook and twitter will attract millions of new users but reap only slender revenues</s>
<s>nanotechnology and genome research will yield fresh marvels</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the trade spats of 2009 will not with luck escalate into a fullblown trade war but protectionist sentiment will rise</s>
<s>governments that cooperated effectively to contain the financial crisis will have to cooperate again to keep trade flowing</s>
<s>much will also depend on chinas continued willingness to lend money to a stillprofligate uncle sam</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>president barack obama wants to raise taxes to ease americas budget deficit but he has promised to soak only the rich</s>
<s>he will wait until after the midterm elections in november 2010 to break this promise</s>
<s>if american growth stays slow the public debt burden will become unsustainable and inflation will revive</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>public distrust of business will remain high</s>
<s>to allay it firms will have to become more transparent</s>
<s>and they will have to adapt to a world where big changes happen quickly</s>
<s>bad publicity travels at the speed of light</s>
<s>a big scandal can destroy a firm in an instant</s>
<s>the collapse of one financial enterprise can threaten the whole system</s>
<s>governments cannot stop every foundering company from going under nor should they</s>
<s>so firms will need sharp reflexes in 2010 tempered by caution</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>robert guest washington correspondent the economist</s>
</p>
