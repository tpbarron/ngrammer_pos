<p>
<s>mobile operators know who you call banks know what you buy supermarkets know what you eat</s>
<s>transactional data helps businesses make money and the government thinks consumers should profit from it too</s>
<s>last year the business department encouraged companies to give people digital copies of information held about them through a voluntary scheme called midata</s>
<s>it now wants to force firms to cough up the files</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>existing laws require companies to reply to requests for personal data but only on paper</s>
<s>some take 40 days to respond</s>
<s>the government thinks that consumers and the economy would benefit if they did so immediately</s>
<s>price comparison engines that suck in itemised telephone bills could find people better deals</s>
<s>personal finance sites empowered to ingest financial statements could help spendthrifts reform</s>
<s>in the long term the government hopes entrepreneurs will dream up more inventive datadriven services</s>
<s>startups able to track a user’s purchases might offer to store receipts and warranties in a central location for a fee</s>
<s>digital wardrobes might catalogue a shopper’s new clothes then recommend accessories</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>britain’s energy companies are championing the scheme</s>
<s>finance telecoms and retail firms are less enthused</s>
<s>more demanding data rules will create additional costs they argue</s>
<s>companies do not want competitors using data they have paid to collect</s>
<s>martin hoskins a dataprotection consultant worries that intermediaries will encourage consumers to hand over large volumes of personal information without clear limits on its use</s>
<s>a government consultation ends on september 10th</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>britain is already “streets ahead” of most countries in liberating consumer data says liz brandt of ctrlshift a marketing consultancy</s>
<s>but other places are catching up</s>
<s>the websites of some american healthcare providers now feature a blue button that lets patients download details of their treatment</s>
<s>energy companies there are trialling a green button that works the same way</s>
<s>a draft eu law which could come into force by 2015 gives all citizens the right to move their data between suppliers</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>farsighted optimists spy a broader technological revolution with britain in the vanguard</s>
<s>if a clutch of firms that understand how to manage and crunch personal data emerge britain will be able to export its knowhow across the world</s>
<s>that of course depends on persuading domestic consumers that personal data are something they can use—and sell to others</s>
<s>in may a report from the world economic forum declared that data locked in servers are like “money hidden under a mattress”</s>
<s>yet many people still think personal data are more like personal odour—involuntary embarrassing and best not shared at all</s>
</p>
