<p>
<s>since 2003 the international finance corporation ifc and the world bank have been tracking the businessfriendliness of government rules around the world</s>
<s>things are looking up</s>
<s>nearly all regions are catching up with the best practices seen in the richest countries</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>this matters for many reasons</s>
<s>one is that onerous rules breed corruption</s>
<s>for as many countries as it can the ifc plots its own measures of the regulatory burden against perceived levels of corruption as ranked by transparency international a pressure group</s>
<s>as the chart shows the more rules impede business the more incentive businessfolk have to bribe them away</s>
<s>lighter rules mean less baksheesh</s>
<s>they also mean a larger formal economy and a wider tax base</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>in “doing business 2013” published this week the countries that score well are not those with no regulation at all—somalia is a fearsome place to do business—but places where rules are simple and designed to make markets work better</s>
<s>the top 20 list includes the usual suspects singapore hong kong the nordic countries america</s>
<s>but less obvious entrants are there too georgia malaysia and thailand</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the most dramatic progress has come in making it simpler to jump through the regulatory hoops necessary to start a business</s>
<s>since 2005 the average time it takes has fallen from 50 days to 30</s>
<s>among the worst performers the bottom quartile which are mostly poor countries the improvement has been slightly greater from 112 days to 63</s>
<s>but they still have far to go in new zealand the process takes only one day</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>augusto lopezclaros of the world bank sees growing evidence that better business conditions are boosting economic growth job creation and trade</s>
<s>reducing the time it takes to fill in the forms for shipping goods can lead to a one percent rise in exports he notes</s>
</p>

<p>
<s>the doing business rankings seem to have spurred reform</s>
<s>in 2005 only a third of countries in subsaharan africa were reforming now over twothirds are</s>
<s>poland ukraine and uzbekistan have made big gains</s>
<s>even the sick men of europe—greece and italy—are showing signs of progress</s>
<s>unlike bailouts cutting rules comes cheap</s>
</p>
